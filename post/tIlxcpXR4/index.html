Can't eat skin to replenish collagen?

2020-04-26

  1. Digestion and absorption of collagen (can it be absorbed after eating it?)

  2. Distribution of collagen after absorption (can it reach the skin?)

Summary:

In order to maintain the skin, girls can be described as a hundred treasures. It is also a common maintenance method to eat chicken feet, pig feet, collagen powder and so on to keep the skin tender. But this method of "what to eat and what to make up" to supplement collagen has been repeatedly "refuted" in recent years. It is believed that oral collagen cannot be absorbed by the skin at all.

There are many nutritionists' objections to this view. All in all, they are as follows:

A. Oral collagen is not necessarily absorbed by human body;

Even if it is digested, collagen is first broken down into free amino acids and then absorbed, which is no different from the intake of amino acids in other foods, and does not explain the advantages of collagen compared with other protein sources;

B. Even if collagen is digested and reabsorbed, it cannot reach the skin;

C. Even if it reaches the skin, it can not be guaranteed to participate in the synthesis of collagen in the skin.

In response to the above core questions, some professional scholars answered the latest development of collagen at home and abroad:

  1. Digestion and absorption of collagen (can it be absorbed after eating it?)

Collagen is a kind of easily absorbed collagen, and a considerable part of it is directly absorbed into the blood in the form of polypeptide. Studies have shown that the absorption rate of collagen is more than 95%, even up to 100%. A considerable part of protein macromolecules can be directly absorbed by the intestine. Through 14C labeling, it is proved that the concentration of collagen hydrolysate in the plasma of WINSTAR mice reaches the peak within 3 hours after ingestion of Ch, which indicates that the collagen hydrolysate can be digested and absorbed well.

Some experts have studied the absorption of collagen peptides from pig skin, chicken feet and cartilage in human body. One to two hours after oral administration, it can be absorbed into the blood in large quantities, and it is confirmed that a considerable part (1 / 3 or more) of it is directly absorbed in the form of peptide, rather than just free amino acid (FAA) [9]. Another expert conducted absorption research on collagen from fish scales, fish skin and pig skin, which confirmed the previous results, and found that the most peptide entering the blood was pro Hyp structure. Mariwatanabe Kamiyama et al. (2010) also found that after the mice ingested ch, the characteristic Gly Pro Hyp tripeptide sequence of collagen appeared in their plasma.

Based on previous studies and their own experiments, Steffen oesser, an expert, believes that the idea that protein molecules must be hydrolyzed into free amino acids before they can be absorbed into the blood may need to be revised. Subsequently, many experiments have confirmed that protein molecules can be directly absorbed by the intestine, or even maintain their original functions.

  1. Distribution of collagen after absorption (can it reach the skin?)

The results showed that collagen and its hydrolysate could reach the skin and be used by the skin and liver after being absorbed.

Some foreign experts used 14C radioisotope to trace the distribution of type I collagen ch from chicken feet in mice after absorption. Two to six hours after oral administration, the labeled Gly Pro Hyp tripeptide was distributed to the main organs of the body. After 14 days, only 70% of the radiation level was maintained in the skin. The mouse skin was hydrolyzed and analyzed. It was confirmed that Gly Pro Hyp tripeptide in the mouse skin was tripeptide from chicken feet.

Macrmolecular collagen can be absorbed directly and enriched in kidney and cartilage, while Gly Pro Hyp tripeptide can be deposited in skin specifically.

The results showed that the hydrolysate of type I collagen was initially distributed in the whole body, but it could be targeted in collagen rich tissues (including skin) and used specifically.

To sum up, oral collagen can affect the skin. After all, in China's thousands of years of diet tradition, there have been such delicacies as donkey hide gelatin, flower glue, chicken feet, meat jelly, pig's feet, etc. to improve the skin's elasticity and wrinkle resistance. The main ingredient of these foods is collagen. Modern also has many brands to launch the oral collagen powder, mainly to maintain the skin, keep the skin young.

However, the effect of collagen supplement by oral administration can not be immediate. To keep skin young and tender for a long time, full exercise and sleep is the king!
