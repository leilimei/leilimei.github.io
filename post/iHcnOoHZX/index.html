Have you learned how to prevent and control tuberculosis on the 24th World Tuberculosis Day?

2020-03-31

On March 24, 2019, the 24th World Tuberculosis Day, the theme of which is "to carry out actions to end tuberculosis and build and share a healthy China". In order to improve people's awareness and attention to the harm of tuberculosis, all sectors in China have also made a series of targeted actions.

Nowadays, although many people pay attention to health preservation, the cognition and prevention of some diseases are extremely lacking, and they have never paid attention to it.

In China, there are about 900000 new cases of tuberculosis every year. In order to strengthen people's attention to this disease and improve their TB prevention and control ability, all provinces, cities, prefectures and counties have carried out various forms of publicity and consultation activities to publicize TB prevention and control knowledge to the general public and the relevant free policies of the state, answer various related doubts for the public, and guide everyone to learn how to prevent and control tuberculosis.

Colleges and universities have also responded to the policy by holding awareness campaigns and lectures on TB knowledge in order to improve students' awareness of TB prevention and control.

Tuberculosis seriously affects the health of patients. If not treated thoroughly, it will affect the quality of life, lose the ability to work, and even cause death. An untreated infectious tuberculosis patient can infect 10 to 15 healthy people a year. Therefore, it is necessary for us to learn how to prevent and treat tuberculosis so as not to be harmed by the disease.

Tuberculosis, also known as "tuberculosis", is a disease caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Although it may invade various organs of the human body, it mainly invades the lungs, so it is also called tuberculosis. And tuberculosis is a chronic infectious disease, which can be spread through the respiratory tract between people.

Tuberculosis is an infectious disease. To prevent infectious diseases, we need to control the source of infection, cut off the route of infection and protect the susceptible. An untreated infectious tuberculosis patient can infect 10 to 15 healthy people a year. Therefore, we should pay attention to the following aspects in the prevention of tuberculosis:

  1. Find tuberculosis patients and suspicious symptoms in time and treat them as soon as possible;

  2. Family members and close contacts of tuberculosis patients should receive relevant examination;

  3. Pay attention to the ventilation and environmental sanitation of the residential area;

  4. Pay attention to nutrition and rest, and exercise properly;

  5. BCG vaccination for newborn infants.

The harm of tuberculosis is huge. We must pay attention to the prevention of the disease. Once found, we need to see a doctor in time.

Prevalence of tuberculosis: common symptoms

  1. Pulmonary tuberculosis

Cough and expectoration are the most common early symptoms of pulmonary tuberculosis, with bloodshot or small blood clots in the sputum.

  1. Tuberculosis of stomach

The clinical manifestations are very inconsistent, some are asymptomatic or mild, some are similar to chronic gastritis, gastric cancer, and most are ulcer like diseases. The patients have upper abdominal discomfort or pain, often accompanied by acid regurgitation and belching.

The vomiting of pyloric obstruction is mainly in the afternoon and evening. The vomitus is the food. It does not contain bile. The occult blood can be negative. The abdominal distention is reduced after vomiting.

In addition to stomach symptoms, it can also be accompanied by systemic tuberculosis symptoms, such as fatigue, weight loss, fever in the afternoon, night sweats, etc.

  1. liver tuberculosis

The most common symptoms are fever and fatigue. Other symptoms include anorexia, nausea, vomiting, abdominal distention and diarrhea. Fever usually occurs in the afternoon, sometimes accompanied by chills and night sweats; there are low fever and relaxation type, high fever can reach 39-41 ℃. People suffering from tuberculosis may have recurrent fever for a long time.

  1. intestinal tuberculosis

Most of them have slow onset and long course of disease. If they coexist with extraintestinal tuberculosis, their clinical manifestations can be covered and ignored.
