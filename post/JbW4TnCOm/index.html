Firstly, it is emphasized that oligopeptide-1 ≠ human oligopeptide-1 (EGF)

Oligopeptide-1 ≈ Tripeptide-1 (3 peptide), human oligopeptide-1 = EGF (53 peptide), but oligopeptide-1 ≠ human oligopeptide-1 (EGF). EGF is not what we see as "oligopeptide-1". No matter from the number of amino acids and biological activity, there is a huge difference, which is basically two different substances. Amino acid sequence of EGF

The chemical structure formula of Tripeptide-1 (oligopeptide-1) believes that the topic of oligopeptide-1 and EGF is still a hot topic in the near future, especially after the State Drug Administration clearly pointed out that oligopeptide-1 and human oligopeptide-1 (epidermal growth factor, EGF) are not the same substance on the official website on January 10, 2019, it also set off a big wave of vibration in the circle of friends, whether it is consumers or the interests in the industrial chain All the related people cried out, "zhongzhao" brand is familiar to everyone.

As for the topic of oligopeptide-1 and EGF, I have written an article last year. You can read it if you are interested. Markgo: before the official documents of the drug regulatory bureau came out, the "God" oligopeptide-1 touted in the industry was indeed EGF (53 peptide), but the real oligopeptide-1 should be Tripeptide-1 in strict sense, just like the common name of EGF (human oligopeptide-1), so it has been used as the vest of EGF, But epidermal growth factor has never been approved for use.

When oligopeptide-1 was born, the real ingredient was palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 (palmitoyl oligopeptide), which can be seen in many famous classic products. However, the word oligopeptide is relatively vague, and it has been gradually eliminated in the world. It has long been renamed as Tripeptide-1. There is a certain lag in this aspect in China, so the name (oligopeptide-1) is still in use, but it will be eliminated sooner or later in China.

After the official document of the drug regulatory bureau came out, many brands in the industry were in a hurry to delete posts, remove products from the shelves, modify the product details page and find the real source of "oligopeptide-1". So whether it's the halo of Nobel prize or the former "executives" of Ali, although "oligopeptide-1" is written in the product, if the real "oligopeptide-1" is installed, there is no problem in long-term use, but the EGF installed has potential safety risks, so how to select it without a long-term use of the speaker's office depends on everyone's insight and the next rectification efforts of the drug administration.

At present, EGF is only used as a medical external preparation for the treatment of skin burn wounds (shallow π - deep Ⅱ degree burn wounds), residual wounds, donor area wounds and chronic ulcer wounds, or for micro needle, laser and other postoperative repair treatment. However, there are some hidden dangers in the application of EGF in cosmetics. Abuse of EGF may lead to uncontrollable skin growth and "tumor like" nodular hyperplasia. The abuse of EGF injection, resulting in excessive growth of skin tissue is often reported.
