Guide reading

In 2016, two peptide drugs were approved for marketing, namely, etelcalcetide of Amgen and folitropin delta of Huiling. Up to now, more than 100 peptide drugs have been approved. In 2015, the global peptide drug market exceeded $20 billion, and it is predicted that it will exceed $25 billion in 2018. China is a big peptide consumer with a terminal market of more than 30 billion yuan, but it is not a strong country. Most of China's peptide market is divided by multinational pharmaceutical companies.

With the development of new drugs, the development of small molecular drugs is becoming more and more difficult, and the market share of small molecular drugs is also constantly eroded by biological products. Among the top ten products in global sales, there are few small molecular drugs left. We can no longer confine our eyes to the field of small molecules. The achievements of microRNA and small peptide (3-50 amino acids) in foreign countries are obvious to all. They will break out in the next decade, and we will see them here.

So far, more than 7000 natural peptides have been identified, and these peptides play a key role in human physiology, including hormones, neurotransmitters, growth factors, ion channel ligands, or anti infection. It is predicted that the global peptide drug market will increase from US $14.1 billion in 2011 to about US $25.4 billion in 2018. Among them, the innovative peptide drug market will increase from $8.6 billion in 2011 to about $17 billion in 2018.

At present, nearly 100 peptide products have been approved for marketing in the world (see Table 1 for details). In 2015, the total global sales reached nearly 22 billion US dollars, accounting for about 2% of the total score of the medical and pharmaceutical market. Peptide medicine is a kind of medicine with rapid market growth, with a compound growth rate of 10.80% in ten years, and the total sales volume has tripled from 2006 to 2015. According to the ranking of total sales in 2014, there are one with more than $4 billion, three with more than $2 billion and seven with more than $1 billion (see Table 2 for details).

Gratire

Graticule was approved by FDA in 1996 and used in the treatment of multiple sclerosis. Among many peptide products, gratiray is the leader, with global sales of US $4.2 billion in 2014, accounting for 20% of multiple sclerosis market. However, with the expiration of the patent and the competition of MS heavy-duty product dimethyl fumarate, the market share of gratire is about to decline. In 2015, the global sales volume of MS drugs has exceeded 21 billion US dollars, but the domestic market is not promising. According to the literature, there are only about 30000 MS patients in China, and the market is relatively limited.

Glucagon like peptide analogue (GLP)

GLP analogues include GLP-1 analogues and GLP-2 analogues. GLP-1 analogues are mainly used in type 2 diabetes mellitus. The drugs on the market include exenatide, lilalutide, lisnertide, dulaglutide and abirotide. GLP-2 analogues are used in short bowel syndrome, and only tiludulin is available.

GLP-1 analogues are a hot research field of peptides. The length of peptide chain is basically between 31-44 amino acids, and there is no disulfide bond in the structure. In 2015, the global sales of GLP-1 analogues reached US $3.9 billion. Some analysts predict that the market of GLP-1 analogues will exceed US $6 billion in five years. According to the data released by relevant companies, the total sales volume of dulaglutide, lisneretide and abirotide just listed in 2015 has reached US $350 million, and the growth is strong. This kind of products can not only reduce blood sugar unilaterally, but also can be combined with insulin, and can also be used to lose weight. Therefore, the sales volume of this kind of products will increase rapidly in the next five years. Not only that, but also many products have been in the late clinical stage, the most noteworthy is novo oral somalutide.

GLP-2 analogues are currently only sold by titurotide, and there is no recent listing in the research project. Because they are orphan drugs, the market competition is relatively small, and they are exclusive products, laughing for a few money. According to the data released by shire (acquisition of NPs), the sales of titurotide in 2013, 2014 and 2015 were 31 million US dollars, 68 million US dollars and 144 million US dollars respectively.

Growth hormone vs somatostatin and its analogues

Growth hormone and somatostatin are a pair of opposite substances, which are used to treat various diseases caused by the imbalance of growth hormone secretion. Growth hormone and somatostatin are relatively old products, which are produced by many manufacturers. According to incomplete statistics, the global sales volume of growth hormone in 2014 was about 2.8 billion US dollars, and the main manufacturers were Roche, Pfizer, Novo Nordisk, Merck, cherrano, Lilly, ispen and Sumitomo. In terms of somatostatin, the global sales volume is about US $2.4 billion, and the main products are Novartis octreotide, Pfizer's PEG modified somatostatin and ispen's Lanrui peptide.

Luteinizing hormone releasing hormone analogue

These drugs are mainly used to treat prostate cancer, postmenopausal breast cancer, central precocious puberty, endometriosis and other diseases. The main products are goserelin, leuprorelin, triptorelin, buserelin, histamine and gonarelin. According to my statistics, this kind of products almost entered the platform period after 2008, with little change in sales. The total global sales in 2014 was about 5 billion US dollars.

Echinocandins

Echinocandin products are mainly polypeptide antifungal drugs, and the listed drugs include caspofungin, micafungin and anifenin. All three products are deep antifungal drugs, with a total sales volume of about US $1.12 billion in 2015. At present, the research and development of antifungal drugs are slow, and these three drugs have become the mainstay of anti systemic fungi.

Opportunities and challenges in peptide field

Peptide medicine is a huge family, and it is also one of the more promising development directions. Globally, peptide drugs have a market of more than 20 billion US dollars, and it is expected to exceed 25 billion US dollars in 2018. In recent years, the development of peptide drugs has extended many fields of disease treatment, including anti infection, anti-tumor, physiological regulation, pain, heart failure, osteoporosis, diabetes, vaccines and so on. However, most peptide drugs have the characteristics of direct oral ineffectiveness, short biological half-life and long treatment cycle. Therefore, to improve the compliance of patients, it is also a very meaningful thing to re develop the existing peptide drugs. At present, there are two ways to improve the peptide drugs in foreign countries: long-term injection products, such as preparation of polypeptide liposomes, polypeptide microspheres, polyethylene glycol modification, fusion protein or subcutaneous implantation, bypass injection, oral administration, transdermal administration and inhalation administration, etc.

The resources of generic drugs are very limited, and the resources of three kinds of drugs are even more precious. Parody has been the hottest word in recent years, but most of them are small molecule drugs, while large molecule drugs are ignored, mainly because of the technical barriers that are hard to break. At present, except for glutathione, thymopentin, leuprorelin and octreotide, all polypeptides sold in China are imported, and the market share of imported products is nearly 80%. At present, peptide synthesis in China is still in its infancy, and the products that can be produced independently in China are all short peptides with no more than 10 amino acids. The research and development of peptide products is different from that of generic drugs, which requires a little accumulation and long-term challenges. If we still do the three kinds of drugs as before, it is difficult to get results. At present, most of the peptides on the market are short peptides with less than 40 amino acids. The difficulty of solid-phase synthesis is impurity control and purification. Peptide synthesis generally has a long process cycle. Unlike the three or five steps of general medicine, peptide synthesis generally involves dozens of steps of chemical reactions. In the middle, it may also need several times of freeze-drying and purification. When it comes to purification, I get drunk as soon as I see it. As soon as I open the preparation, acetonitrile flows at a rate of several liters per minute, so the impurity control is not good, and the purification cannot be carried out.

At present, the biggest difficulty is that there are too few people who know peptide synthesis technology and the equipment cannot keep up with it. The so-called team is the elite of peptide synthesis, the expert of purification and analysis, the expert of preparation and process transformation. In addition, one of the major problems in the development of peptide drugs is that there are few products and the preparation process is complex, so it is difficult for different products to be produced in the same production line, even if they can be produced in the same line, it is difficult to achieve the same line sales, so high cost and accurate project approval are crucial.
