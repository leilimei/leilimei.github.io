Modulatory effect of small molecule active peptide on arteriosclerosis

Abstract:

The report shows that the incidence rate of arteriosclerosis obliterans in China is as high as 12%-20%.

When it comes to arteriosclerosis, many people will think that it is the disease of the elderly. In fact, the incidence of arteriosclerosis obliterans generally starts from the young age, starting with the hardening of the blood vessel wall, then the thickening of the vascular lumen, and finally the rupture of the blood vessel after a certain age. This is the evolution process of arteriosclerosis obliterans.

The cause of arteriosclerosis

\1. Hyperlipidemia:

the amount of fat in the blood is too high, which is easy to deposit on the inner wall of the blood vessels to form plaque, resulting in arteriosclerosis and stenosis.

\2. Hypertension:

high pressure blood flow impacts the arterial wall for a long time, causing mechanical damage to the arterial intima, causing deposition of lipids in the arterial wall, forming fatty plaque, and causing arteriosclerosis and stenosis. If blood pressure is not controlled, the incidence of myocardial infarction will increase by 2-3 times, and stroke by 4 times.

\3. Diabetes mellitus:

there will be problems in fat metabolism of diabetic patients. The protein (called lipoprotein) that transports fat in the blood will have denaturation. In the process of transportation, fat is easy to deposit on the inner wall of blood vessels, forming fat plaque.

\4. Smoking: nicotine, carbon monoxide, etc.

in cigarettes will damage the inner wall of arteries. The inner wall of injured arteries will block cholesterol, cause platelet accumulation, and form fat plaque. Meanwhile, smoking will also cause coronary vasoconstriction and spasm, and reduce blood flow.

\5. Lack of exercise: exercise can increase high-density lipoprotein, reduce low-density lipoprotein,

help the body to remove excess cholesterol from the biliary tract and intestines, so as to avoid excess cholesterol deposition on the inner wall of blood vessels. In addition, exercise can promote blood circulation, increase the elasticity of blood vessels, reduce blood pressure, consume excess heat to reduce the proportion of body fat, increase the proportion of muscle and lose weight. Therefore, people who lack exercise are easy to get congee arteriosclerosis.

\6. Obesity:

the heart load of obese or overweight people increases, and the probability of abnormal blood fat is also higher, thus increasing the risk of atherosclerotic congee. Obesity is easy to cause hypertension, diabetes, hyperlipidemia and insulin resistance syndrome.

The harm of arteriosclerosis

\1. Atherosclerosis of the aorta and its branches can lead to the decrease of the elasticity of the vascular wall, affect the systemic blood flow regulation, increase the systolic pressure, widen the pulse pressure and increase the burden on the heart.

\2. The stenosis or occlusion of the internal organs and the arterial lumen of the extremities due to atherosclerosis may lead to the decrease of the blood supply to the organs and tissues, resulting in the ischemia and necrosis of the corresponding organs and tissues.

\3. Coronary atherosclerosis results in stenosis or occlusion of the lumen, which leads to the insufficiency of myocardial blood supply, and leads to myocardial ischemia, hypoxia, angina, myocardial infarction and other heart diseases.

\4. Cerebral atherosclerosis, can lead to the lack of blood supply to the brain, serious cases can lead to cerebral infarction, cerebral hemorrhage or brain atrophy.

\5. Renal atherosclerosis can lead to unstable hypertension.

\6. Mesenteric atherosclerosis can cause dyspepsia, constipation or abdominal pain.

\7. Atherosclerosis of the lower extremities can cause coldness, numbness, intermittent falls, gangrene and so on.
