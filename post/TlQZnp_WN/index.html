Research shows that anti-aging compounds

2020-05-06

"Rejuvenate" may become possible

Individual aging is accompanied by the gradual decline of various biological functions of the body, one of the important characteristics is the accumulation of aging cells in the body. The dysfunction of aging cells and the secretion of a variety of inflammatory factors and cytokines will cause the dysfunction of tissues and organs and accelerate the aging process.

Therefore, selective elimination of senescent cells in vivo has become an important direction of anti-aging research. It has been reported that the compounds for eliminating senescent cells can induce senescent cell death mainly by inhibiting the anti apoptotic pathway of cells.

However, these compounds have obvious limitations. First of all, the broad-spectrum is not strong, different types of aging cells activate different anti apoptotic pathways, so targeting small molecules of specific anti apoptotic pathways can not effectively remove many types of aging cells in vivo; second, the specificity is not high, such compounds can activate the apoptotic signaling pathway of aging cells, but also "mistakenly kill" non aging cells.

Therefore, how to specifically remove the aging cells accumulated in various tissues and organs is a key problem.

On April 27, Deng Hongkui's research group of School of life sciences of Peking University and Luo tuoping's research group of School of chemistry and Molecular Engineering published the research progress of aging cells online in the Journal of cell research. According to the important characteristics of aging cells, the research group cleverly designed and synthesized a new compound, which proved that the compound can effectively remove the aging cells accumulated in multiple tissues and organs of aging individuals, significantly improve the physiological state of aging mice, and restore the body functions of aging mice.

In old mice, ssk1 can be selectively activated by β - gal cleavage of aging cells, specifically scavenge aging cells in vivo, reduce aging related characteristics, alleviate chronic inflammation, and restore the physiological function of old mice. Photo source: School of life sciences, Peking University

In this work, the research team established a new targeted strategy to remove aging cells. In this strategy, a novel anti-aging compound ssk1, which can be specifically activated by the metabolism of SA - β - gal in senescent cells, is designed and synthesized by utilizing the most typical characteristic of senescent cells, i.e. enhanced activity of lysosomal β - gal. Ssk1 itself has no killing effect. When ssk1 enters into senescent cells, the β - galactoside bond of ssk1 will be rapidly cut by SA - β - gal, releasing toxic molecules with killing effect and inducing senescent cells to die; when ssk1 enters into non senescent cells, due to the low activity of β - gal, ssk1 will not be activated by metabolism and will not have killing effect.

The team first confirmed through cell experiments in vitro that ssk1 can effectively remove various types of aging cells without affecting the survival of normal cells, showing the broad-spectrum and specificity of the new small molecular compound in removing aging cells.

They further verified in vivo the effect of the new small molecular compound on scavenging senescent cells and anti-aging.

First of all, ssk1 can significantly reduce the number of senescent cells in multiple tissues and organs of aging mice, and reverse the aging related gene expression profile.

Secondly, ssk1 significantly reduced the secretion of many inflammatory factors and cytokines (SASP) by senescent cells in tissues and blood system, and alleviated the chronic inflammation related to aging.

Further systematic functional tests showed that after ssk1 treatment, many aging related functional degradation (decreased exercise tolerance, muscle strength, balance ability and spontaneous exploration) were significantly improved in the elderly mice, and the effect was significantly better than the reported compounds for eliminating aging cells. The research team also systematically tested the safety of ssk1 in vivo, and found that even high doses of ssk1 had no obvious side effects.

This research work has proved for the first time the specificity and broad-spectrum of a new generation of small molecular compound ssk1 designed for β - galactosidase of aging cells, which can effectively slow down the aging symptoms of old mice, significantly improve the physiological function of old mice, and provide a new way for the research and development of anti-aging drugs.

This work was supported by NSFC, national key R & D program, and Peking University Tsinghua Life Science Joint Center. (source: Zhang Qingdan, China Science Daily)
