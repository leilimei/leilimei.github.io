The origin of World Hepatitis Day

In 2004, the World Health Organization held its first World Hepatitis Day campaign to disseminate knowledge about hepatitis C prevention, screening and treatment to the public, the medical community and government officials.

In 2010, in order to commemorate Baruch Samuel Blumberg, the discoverer of hepatitis B virus, the World Health Organization decided to designate July 28 as World Hepatitis Day from 2011.

Because Bloomberg has made substantial progress in the search for the pathogenic microorganisms that cause hepatitis B, which has made little progress in the past 20 years.

Baruch Samuel Bloomberg:

American physician, winner of the 1976 Nobel Prize in physiology and medicine.

In the 1960s, when Bloomberg studied the relationship between genetic polymorphism of serum antigens and disease susceptibility, he and his collaborators began to consider the relationship between Australian antigens (now known as HBsAg) and hepatitis.

It was suggested that there is a close relationship between Australian antigens and acute viral hepatitis, which may be transmitted by blood transfusion, thus winning the Nobel Prize in physiology or medicine in 1976.

After that, we successfully developed a hepatitis B vaccine product, which was prepared by purifying hepatitis B surface antigen from blood, and put it into use in 1981.

This is our first generation of vaccine - blood vaccine.

Hepatitis is around us

According to the information collected by Xiaobian, now hepatitis has become the second largest infectious disease that can lead to death after tuberculosis, and about 2 billion people around the world have been infected with hepatitis virus, and about 325 million people have been affected by hepatitis B and hepatitis C in their normal lives.

In addition, about 1.4 million people will die of hepatitis every year, and 80% of them are lack of prevention, screening and treatment. Many people do not know enough about it. In the modern era of such progress in medicine, they are still searching for the right way, which leads to the delay of the disease.

In China alone, there are about 90 million carriers of hepatitis B virus, of which about 28 million are chronic hepatitis B patients, and about 7.6 million are hepatitis C virus infected, of which about 4.56 million are chronic hepatitis C patients.

Every year, about 330000 people die of liver cirrhosis and primary liver cancer caused by hepatitis B or C infection, which is not a small number. Maybe it happens around us and threatens the lives of people around us, so we need to work together to fight against the virus.
