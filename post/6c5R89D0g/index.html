Progress in self-assembly and energy storage of helix polypeptide

2020-06-08

Recently, Li Zhou research group of Beijing Institute of nano energy and systems, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and Li Zigang research group and Wang Xinwei research group of Shenzhen Graduate School of Peking University have made new progress in the research of new self-assembly mode of α - helix polypeptide and its application in energy storage. Relevant research results have been published in the recent science advance (DOI: 10.1126/ sciadv.aar5907 )。

As a bottom-up method for the preparation of biomaterials, the precise hierarchical self-assembly of polypeptide molecules has attracted more and more attention in the fields of bionics, catalysis, material separation and bioelectronic devices. Compared with inorganic nanomaterials, polypeptide molecules have the characteristics of wide sources, natural degradation, good biocompatibility, easy to modify and so on. With FF (F: Phenylalanine (two) and its derivatives are represented by a series of physical (electric field assisted method, magnetic field assisted method, temperature assisted method, ultrasonic assisted method) and chemical (pH, solvent, catalytic method) methods, which accurately manipulate the path of polypeptide molecular self-assembly, and obtain a series of special junctions such as polypeptide nanotubes nanofibers, nanowires, nanomaterials, and nano quantum dots. These materials either have good semiconductor properties or exhibit superior flexibility and special optical properties. It has been widely used in the fields of flexible super capacitor / battery, piezoelectric device, biosensor, photoconductive device and biomedicine.

Polypeptide self-assembly is a means to realize long-range ordered arrangement of polypeptide molecules under certain conditions by regulating non covalent interactions among polypeptide molecules, such as van der Waals force, hydrogen bond network, hydrophobic interaction, π - π interaction, etc. Because of the complexity and diversity of polypeptide molecular structure, the change of polypeptide sequence leads to different molecular properties. Therefore, it is very difficult to predict and design peptide self-assembly. In the early years, the research on peptide self-assembly mainly focused on amphiphilic peptide, β - folding peptide, ring-d-l-d peptide and FF dipeptide. For more complex self-assembly of helix peptides, there are few reports. The reason is that the entropy of helix peptide is low, and the hydrogen bond network is blocked in the peptide, so it is difficult to form the hydrogen bond network between molecules. Therefore, how to manipulate the peptides with more complex secondary structure, such as helix peptides, to achieve controllable self-assembly, is one of the most important and difficult problems in current research.

Li Zhou research group has long paid attention to and published a series of preparation and application of implantable devices based on biodegradable molecules. In view of the great prospect of peptide molecules in this field, the research group has carried out the research of peptide nanomaterials in the field of energy storage and sensing, and has published relevant papers in the international academic journal small. Recently, Li Zhou group and its collaborators have developed a new method for the preparation of peptide nanomaterials based on the chiral induced helix system. Based on this system, the researchers believe that the lack of self-assembly driving force of the main chain of helix polypeptide can be compensated by the driving force side ring interaction outside the main chain, so as to realize a new pattern of peptide assembly method, namely the so-called "side ring driven" peptide self-assembly. Based on such a concept, the researchers conducted a detailed screening and characterization. They designed and synthesized a series of pentapeptide molecules with chiral side ring, named bdcp. The results show that when bdcp is helical and the side ring substituents are aromatic, the peptide molecules can be assembled into nanotubes / nanobelts.
Next, they studied the mechanism of peptide self-assembly. Through the cooperation with two research groups of Shenzhen Graduate School of Peking University, the crystal structure of peptide assembly was predicted, and the assembly model of peptide molecule was obtained. The model shows good agreement with the experimental results. The results show that π - π interaction, s - π interaction and hydrogen bond network drive the assembly of peptide molecules. In the polypeptide assembly, the polar and nonpolar interfaces are formed in turn, and the dipole directions of each two polar layers are opposite, which makes the dipole interactions in the assembly cancel each other and further stabilize the assembly. This is also the first reported example of S - π interaction in peptide self-assembly.

On this basis, the optical and electrical properties of the assembled materials were further studied. Under different excitation light, the peptide assembly can emit fluorescence from blue to red. This material has a great application prospect in the field of biological imaging. In the electrical test, under the guidance of Li Zhou, the researchers explored the energy storage properties of polypeptide assembly as the active material of supercapacitor. In 2009, scientists from Israel first reported the application of cyclic phenylalanine dipeptide "nanoforest" material in energy storage. The research shows that the polypeptide material has good mechanical and electrical properties. Combined with the advantages of high specific surface area, suitable hydrophobicity, and excellent conductivity of the polypeptide nano material, the polypeptide energy storage has become a potential option for the next generation of flexible, implantable, light and pollution-free energy storage devices. In this study, we systematically compared the electrochemical properties of four groups of peptide molecules, and the results showed that the energy storage of peptide materials can be controlled by peptide sequence. By means of cyclic voltammetry, constant current charge discharge and other electrochemical measurements, it is proved that the polypeptide material has good cyclic stability, multiplying power and higher specific capacitance than FF polypeptide material.

This research not only has important theoretical value in peptide material preparation, but also has important application prospects. Hu Kuan, an assistant researcher of Li Zhou research group, is the co-author of the paper. Li Hu, a doctoral student, has contributed to the electrochemical test. On the basis of the above work, Li Zhou research group is actively carrying out the research work of polypeptide energy storage and polypeptide piezoelectric sensor.
