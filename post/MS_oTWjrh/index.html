What is chronic hepatitis B?

Chronic hepatitis B (abbreviated as hepatitis B) refers to those who are positive for hepatitis B virus, whose course of disease is more than half a year, or whose onset date is not clear and whose clinical manifestations are chronic hepatitis.

The clinical manifestations are asthenia, fear of food, nausea, abdominal distention, pain in the liver area and other symptoms. The liver is large, with medium hardness and light tenderness. Patients with severe disease may be accompanied by chronic liver disease, facial features, spider nevus, liver palms, spleen enlargement, and abnormal or persistent liver function.

According to the clinical manifestations, it can be divided into mild, moderate and severe. Chronic hepatitis B carrier refers to those who are positive for hepatitis B virus test and have no symptoms of chronic hepatitis, who have been followed up for more than three times in a year without abnormal serum ALT and AST, and whose liver histology is normal.

2 etiology

Chronic hepatitis B is caused by the infection of hepatitis B virus (HBV). Hepatitis B patients and HBV carriers are the main source of infection.

HBV can be transmitted through mother and infant, blood and blood products, damaged skin and mucous membrane and sexual contact. After HBV infection, there will be different outcomes and clinical types due to the influence of virus factors, host factors and environmental factors.

The common causes leading to the development of chronic hepatitis B are: familial transmission of infectious virus in infancy, lack of awareness of prevention, missed diagnosis, low immune function and previous history of other liver diseases.

3 hazards

The harm of chronic hepatitis B is not small, not only affects the health of patients, but also interferes with their normal life, and causes great psychological harm. Hepatitis B virus exists in the body for a long time, which will continuously damage the liver. The course of disease can develop to cirrhosis, liver cancer, life-threatening. Chronic hepatitis B may also cause other diseases - chronic hepatitis B complications.

Generally speaking, hepatitis B is a silent disease, which happens a lot quietly. Many patients even have cirrhosis and don't know if they have tumors. Secondly, it is a disease that keeps accumulating, so it is very important to check regularly, at least once every six months, to prevent the occurrence of cancer.

4 key points of treatment

The key point of chronic hepatitis B treatment is antiviral treatment, and the purpose of treatment is to inhibit the replication of hepatitis B virus for a long time, slow down liver inflammation and fibrosis, delay liver failure, liver cirrhosis decompensation, and the occurrence of primary liver cancer. So as to improve the quality of life and prolong the survival time. Some patients can achieve the surface antigen turn negative through treatment, and the liver function can return to normal.
