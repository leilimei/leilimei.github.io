Abstract:

With the continuous development of modern medicine, viruses and diseases should gradually be in the "downwind" in theory, but the actual situation is the opposite. In recent years, SARS, Ebola and other new viruses emerge in an endless stream, and these increasingly complex and difficult to eliminate viruses continue to threaten human health.

At present, the root causes of various viruses are as follows:

\1. Virus variation

In modern medicine, the abuse of chemical drugs, especially antibiotics, makes the virus "upgrade".

\2. The general decline of human immune function

Modern people's life and diet are irregular, lack of exercise, pesticide residues, transgenic, land and air pollution are weakening people's resistance.

Therefore, to improve the immune ability of modern people, we must use modern new immune weapons to deal with the modern variant influenza virus. The strength of human immune function, that is, the strength of resistance, mainly depends on the number of immune cells in the human body, and immune cells are closely related to peptides.

Why do people lack peptide?

(1) Primary malnutrition. Due to the low protein content or poor protein quality of food, after human digestion and degradation, the small peptide protein obtained is less, and the various nutrients contained in the small peptide are deficient.

(2) Secondary malnutrition. The human body's ability to degrade protein, that is to say, the ability to digest protein is poor, and the absorption is also poor, that is to say, secondary to some diseases, resulting in the poor ability to synthesize peptide in the body, poor absorption, improper use, or excessive discharge.

Expression of peptide deficiency

Peptide malnutrition is a serious nutritional deficiency, which is characterized by emaciation, edema and fatigue. clinical manifestation:

(1) Emaciated type, severe weight loss, disappearance of subcutaneous tissue, serious loss of muscle in the whole body, similar to a skeleton.

(2) edema type hair coloring, characterized by edema, muscle wasting, splenomegaly, liver, liver function decline, low resistance, bacterial infection morbidity and mortality increased.

(3) Fatigue type, performance for sleepiness, sleep is not good, trance, chest tightness, shortness of breath, uncomfortable and so on.

In general, the immune function of people with peptide malnutrition is lower than normal. The details are as follows:

\1. Thymus and lymph nodes: thymus and lymph nodes are the first organs and tissues to suffer from polypeptide malnutrition. The thymus was reduced in size, weight, cortex and medulla, and cell number. The size, weight, tissue structure, cell density and composition of spleen and lymph nodes also showed obvious degeneration. If accompanied by infection, the lymphoid tissue is further atrophied. The results showed that the thymus tissue could return to normal after peptide nutrition was given to the animals lacking peptide nutrition.

\2. Cellular immunity: cellular immunity refers to immunity caused by T lymphocytes. In the absence of peptide nutrition, thymus and other tissues atrophy, and the growth of T cells is affected. The decrease of cellular immune function is not only manifested in the decrease of T cell number, but also in the dysfunction of function.

\3. Humoral immunity: humoral immunity refers to the immunity caused by B lymphocytes. When the human body lacks peptide protein nutrition, the number of B cells in the peripheral blood has almost no change. Functional experiments show that no matter the degree of peptide nutrition disorder, the serum concentration is normal or slightly higher, especially with infection, and the production of immunoglobulin is less affected in the absence of peptide, so it is of great significance to the defense function of antibody.

\4. Complement system: complement has the function of promoting immune response, including the effect on conditioning, immune attachment, phagocytosis, chemotaxis of leukocytes and neutralization of virus. In the absence of peptide protein, the total complement and C3 are in critical level or decreased, and the activity is decreased. This is because the synthesis rate of complement is decreased, and the consumption of complement is increased when the infection causes the combination of antigens.

\5. Phagocytes: in patients with severe peptide protein deficiency, the total number of neutrophils remains unchanged, but their functions remain unchanged. The chemotaxis of the cells was normal or slightly slowed down, and the phagocytic activity was normal, but the killing ability of the microorganisms swallowed in the cells was weakened. If the peptide was added in time, the phagocyte function could recover gradually after one or two weeks.

\6. Other immune systems: in the absence of peptide active nutrition, there are also significant changes in some non-specific defense abilities, such as the decrease of lysozyme activity in plasma, tears, saliva and other secretions, the tissue deformation of mucosal epithelial cells, the change of mucosal supplement and ciliary movement, and the decrease of interferon production, which can affect the host's susceptibility to infection.
