Solid phase synthesis of polypeptide

2020-07-14

Peptide synthesis is a process of repeatedly adding amino acids. The solid-phase synthesis sequence is generally from C-terminal (carboxyl end) to N-terminal (amino terminal). Solid phase synthesis greatly reduces the difficulty of each step of product purification. In order to prevent side effects, the side chains of amino acids involved in the reaction are protected.

The carboxyl end is free and must be activated before the reaction. There are two methods of solid state synthesis. Because Fmoc has many advantages over tboc, Fmoc method is mostly used now

Solid phase synthesis of polypeptide

The most important feature of solid-phase synthesis separated from other technologies is solid-phase carrier. The solid-phase carrier that can be used for peptide synthesis must meet the following requirements: it must contain reaction sites (or reaction groups) so that the peptide chain can be connected to these sites and removed later;

it must be stable to the physical and chemical conditions in the synthesis process; the carrier must allow for the continuous growth of peptide chain and peptide chain In addition, the carrier must be allowed to provide enough connection points so that each unit volume of the carrier gives a useful yield of peptide, and the interaction between peptide chains bound by the carrier must be minimized.

There are three kinds of polymer carriers used in the solid-state synthesis of polypeptides: Polystyrene polyethylene cross-linked resin, polyacrylamide, polyethylene glycol resin and derivatives. These resins can be directly linked to (the first) amino acid only by introducing reaction groups. According to the different reaction groups introduced, these resins and resin derivatives can be divided into chloromethyl resin, carboxyl resin, amino resin or hydrazide resin.

BOC synthesis method usually chooses chloromethyl resin, such as Merrifield resin; Fmoc synthesis method usually selects carboxyl resin such as Wang's resin. The immobilization of amino acids is mainly realized by protecting the covalent bond formed between the carboxyl group of amino acid and the reaction group of resin. There are many ways to form covalent bond:

chloromethyl resin usually first produces tetramethylammonium salt or sodium salt, potassium salt and cesium salt to protect amino acid, and then directly reacts with resin at appropriate temperature or in suitable organic solvents such as dioxane, DMF or D For carboxyl resin, appropriate condensation agent such as DCC or carboxydiimidazole is usually added to form co ester of protected amino acid and resin to complete amino acid fixation; while for amino resin or hydrazide resin, amino acid is fixed by protecting amide bond formed between amino acid and resin after adding appropriate condensation agent such as DCC.

Protecting editors

Solid phase synthesis of polypeptide

In order to successfully synthesize peptides with a specific amino acid sequence, it is necessary to protect the amino and carboxyl groups which are not involved in the formation of amide bonds, and to protect the active genes on the side chain of amino acids. After the reaction, the protective genes should be removed. As in liquid phase synthesis, alkoxycarbonyl group is often used as the protective group of α - amino group in solid phase synthesis, because racemization is not easy to occur. Benzyloxycarbonyl was first used.

Because it requires strong acidolysis conditions, it was later changed to BOC protection and TFA (trifluoroacetic acid) protection. However, it is not suitable for the synthesis of peptides with unstable acid, such as tryptophan. Changmeienlofer and Atherton et al. Used Fmoc (9-fluorene methoxycarbonyl) reported by carpino as α - amino protection group. Fmoc group is stable to acid, but can be removed by piperidine-ch2cl2 or piperidine-dmf. In recent years, Fmoc synthesis has been widely used. Carboxyl groups are usually protected by the formation of ester groups. Methyl ester and ethyl ester are commonly used methods to protect carboxyl groups in step-by-step synthesis, which can be removed by saponification or converted into hydrazine for fragment combination; tert butyl ester is removed under acidic conditions;

benzyl ester is usually removed by catalytic hydrogenation. For the synthesis of peptides containing cysteine, histidine, arginine and other amino acids with side chain functional groups, in order to avoid the side effects caused by side chain functional groups, it is generally necessary to protect the side chain groups with appropriate protection groups temporarily.

The selection of protective groups should not only ensure that the side chain groups do not participate in the reaction of amide formation, but also ensure that they are not damaged in the process of peptide synthesis, and can be removed in the final peptide chain cleavage. If triphenylmethyl is used to protect cysteine s -, it is removed by acid or silver salt or mercury salt; the imidazole ring of histidine is protected by 2,2,

2-trifluoro-1-benzyloxycarbonyl and 2,2,2-trifluoro-1-tert-butoxycarbonyl ethyl, which can be removed by catalytic hydrogenation or cold trifluoroacetic acid. Arginine was protected with adamantanoxycarbonyl (ADOC) and removed with cold trifluoroacetic acid.

The principle of peptide reaction in solid phase is basically the same as that in liquid phase. When two corresponding amino acids protected by amino group and protected by carboxyl group are put in solution, peptide bond will not be formed.

To form amide bond, it is often used to activate carboxyl group to form mixed anhydride, active ester, acyl chloride or form symmetrical anhydride with strong losing agent (such as carbodiimide) 。 Among them, DCC, HOBt or HOBt / DCC, symmetrical anhydride method and activated ester method are the most widely used.

TFA + HF was used to cleave and deprotect the side chain in BOC method. TFA was directly used in Fmoc method. Other deprotection methods such as alkali, photolysis, fluorine ion and hydrogenolysis were also used according to different conditions.

High performance liquid chromatography (HPLC), affinity chromatography and capillary electrophoresis (CE) are usually used for further purification, separation and purification of synthetic peptides.
