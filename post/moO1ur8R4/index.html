Manage protein intake in three groups

2020-04-20

  1. Children and adolescents:Protein energy supply ratio is more than 15%

  2. Aged:The older you get, the less protein you eat

  3. Elderly patients:Prognosis is related to protein intake

Abstract:

Protein is the basis of life. Only when the intake of protein is well managed, can the life length be extended and the width of life be widened.

Children and adolescents:Protein energy supply ratio is more than 15%

Children's protein is just like the main building materials needed to build buildings. If the supply is insufficient, the growth and development of children will be directly affected. In serious cases, there will even be problems such as growth retardation, anemia, lack of power, loss of appetite, poor resistance and mental retardation.

Zhang Qian, a researcher at the Institute of nutrition and health, China Center for Disease Control and prevention, said that in 2013, the recommended dietary nutrient intake for children aged 6-18 was about 40-75 grams per day, accounting for about 15% of the total energy intake.

Although protein does not seem to cause much damage to health, foreign studies have suggested that early protein intake in children is related to their growth acceleration or high BMI. Zhang Qian further explained that eating more meat is easy to gain weight, which is understandable for most people, especially for the early high protein intake, such as the high protein intake (energy supply ratio greater than 15%) of children before the age of two, which is related to the risk of obesity. In addition, some studies have found that children's higher intake of animal protein is related to the early puberty and growth acceleration.

Aged:The older you get, the less protein you eat

When you are older, the loss of skeletal muscle in your body increases, and the synthesis efficiency of protein in the elderly is lower than that in the young, especially when you have a lower level of protein, you have a higher synthesis resistance. Therefore, the protein requirement of the elderly is higher than that of the young.

Professor Sun Jianqin's team of clinical nutrition center of East China Hospital Affiliated to Fudan University has investigated the healthy elderly with medium living standards in three communities in Shanghai. Their average intake is 64g for men and 57g for women. More than 40% of the elderly's protein fails to meet the linear recommended quantity. The older the elderly, the more people fail to meet the standard. Moreover, the distribution of protein in three meals is unreasonable. It is less in the morning and at noon, and more in the evening. Professor Sun suggested that the amount of protein in breakfast should be increased. For breakfast, dairy products, bean products, nuts or some protein peptides should be added appropriately (for some old people, it is difficult to let him eat four or two fish, poultry, eggs and milk in one day, if the protein is not enough, protein supplements can be added appropriately). Also It's very effective.

Professor Sun suggested that the elderly should also eat some "hard dishes", that is, meat dishes. It is necessary to eat some lean meat and fish, but the hard vegetables should be soft and chewable, which is not only easier to chew, but also more conducive to absorption. The amino acids in the plasma are much higher. So it's also critical to the way you cook.

Elderly patients:Prognosis is related to protein intake

Under normal circumstances, after eating, the protein of healthy people becomes amino acid and supplies it to all tissues of the body. In the state of disease, patients eat less, and protein is not enough to supply all organs of the body. In this case, the body will convert the protein in bones and skeletal muscles into other organs, which can be used, thus resulting in muscle loss.

According to Professor ardy van of Maastricht University in the Netherlands, 10% of the muscle loss will affect the immune system of patients, which is easy to infect. If 40% of the muscle loss, there is a risk of death.

A large number of clinical research evidence shows that the prognosis of severe patients is closely related to protein intake, and insufficient intake will affect their prognosis and survival period.
