Inventory: top 200 hot drugs in the world in 2019

2020-07-28

Here we are! After a lot of hard work, Xiaotao finally collected the top 200 list of the world's top selling drugs in 2019, mainly covering 16 disease areas, including small molecule inhibitors, monoclonal antibodies, peptides and other molecular drugs. Let's take you to the bottom.

  1. Tumor

Cancer drugs account for nearly a quarter of the world's top 200 drugs list in 2019, indicating its broad market prospects. At present, most of the anti-tumor drugs on the market are small molecule compounds and monoclonal antibodies, with many popular targets, such as PD-1, HER2, CDK4 / 6, Btk, VEGF / VEGFR and so on. Among them, PD-1 inhibitors are the most popular and popular targeted drugs in the tumor market.

Nivolumab（Opdivo, Bristol-Myers Squibb）

Nivolumab is a monoclonal antibody that activates T cells to recognize and kill cancer cells by blocking the PD-1 / PD-L1 pathway. In December 2014, nivolumab was approved by FDA for the treatment of melanoma, becoming the first PD-1 drug approved in the world. After that, nivolumab was successively approved for the treatment of hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) patients who received sorafenib treatment, and advanced, recurrent or metastatic esophageal squamous cell carcinoma (ESCC) patients who received fluorouracil and platinum chemotherapy.

On August 16, 2018, FDA approved lenvatinib as the first-line treatment for patients with unresectable hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC). Lenvatinib is a multi-target receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK) inhibitor, which can inhibit other RTK related to pathological angiogenesis, tumor growth and cancer progression, including fibroblast growth factor (FGF) receptor fgfr1,2,3,4; platelet-derived growth factor receptor α (PDGFR α), kit and ret.

  1. Immune system diseases

Autoimmune disease is a kind of damage disease of tissue and organ caused by abnormal immune system, autoantibodies or autoeffector lymphocytes attacking normal cells. At present, there are about 100 kinds of autoimmune diseases, which are the third major diseases after cardiovascular diseases and tumors. Most of the causes of autoimmune diseases are similar. One treatment drug is often effective for many kinds of autoimmune diseases. In addition, with the support of FDA's rare disease policy, it has become one of the treatment areas that major pharmaceutical companies are keen on in recent years, accounting for 10% of the global top 200 drug list, which can not be ignored.

Adalimumab（Humira，AbbVie）

Since it was approved by FDA for the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis in 2002, the sales of adalimumab (adalimumab) has been increasing year by year, occupying the first place in the global drug sales for six consecutive years, which is worthy of the name of "drug king". It is the first fully humanized monoclonal antibody against tumor necrosis factor - α (TNF - α). It can bind to TNF - α, block the interaction between TNF and cell surface TNF receptors p55 and p75, neutralize TNF activity and reduce MMP-1 concentration. Over the past 18 years, the approved indications include: psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, chronic psoriasis, pyogenic hidrositis and juvenile idiopathic arthritis. Up to now, it has expanded to 14.

Secukinumab（Cosentyx，Novartis）

Secukinumab, a blockbuster IL-17A inhibitor, has been approved by the FDA for the treatment of active non radiologic axial spondyloarthritis (NR axspa). This is the fourth FDA approved indication for sukimumab, which has previously been approved by the FDA for the treatment of psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis. Sujinmab is the first recombinant human IgG1 / κ monoclonal antibody that specifically binds and suppresses IL-17A in the world. It has precise target and higher safety. It shows rapid and lasting efficacy and safety in the treatment of inflammatory diseases.

  1. Central nervous system diseases CNS

In recent years, with the aggravation of the aging population and the increasing prevalence of central nervous system (CNS) diseases, the research and development of such drugs has become a huge market demand. CNS diseases can be divided into six categories: Alzheimer's disease, antidepressants, multiple sclerosis (MS), attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), insomnia drug market and pain medication market.

Guselkumab（Tremfya，J&J）

In December 2017, guselkumab (tenoya) was approved by FDA for the treatment of massive psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis. Compared with the traditional anti-inflammatory antibody targeting TNF, guselkumab specifically acts on IL-23, which is the first and only biotherapy with selective blocking effect on IL-23. Guselkumab plays a key role in the proliferation and differentiation of Th-17 by blocking the IL-23 receptor mediated Th-17 / IL-17 pathway, as well as the pro-inflammatory cytokines including IL-17 and IL-22.

Lurasidone（Latuda，Sunovion）

Lurasidone was approved by FDA in 2010 and 2013 for the treatment of schizophrenia and bipolar type I depression. Lurasidone is an atypical antipsychotic drug, which has antagonistic effects on dopamine D2, 5-HT2A, 5-HT7, 5-HT1A receptors and noradrenalin α 2C.

  1. Diabetes mellitus

Diabetes is a metabolic disease characterized by hyperglycemia. Its main cause is that the pancreas cannot produce enough insulin, or the cells are not sensitive to insulin. In 2014, an estimated 422 million adults were suffering from diabetes. By 2030, diabetes will be the seventh leading cause of death in the world.

Dulaglutide（Trulicity，LLY）

Dulaglutide (dulaglutide), a GLP-1 receptor agonist, was the first diabetic drug to be sold, reaching 4.394 billion. In 2014, it was approved by FDA as a weekly subcutaneous injection for blood glucose control in adults with type 2 diabetes. It has not yet been listed in China. Trulicity's biggest advantage over Victoza, Nord, is to replace daily injections with more convenient weekly injections.

Liraglutide（Victoza，Novo Nordisk）

Liraglutide (liraglutide), which belongs to GLP-1 receptor agonist, was approved by FDA in 2010 for the treatment of adult patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus, and became the first non insulin drug approved for pediatric patients with type 2 diabetes in June 2019. Sales also reached 3.29 billion.

  1. Cardiovascular diseases

Cardiovascular diseases (CVD) is a general term for cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases, which refers to ischemic or hemorrhagic diseases of heart, brain and systemic tissues caused by hyperlipidemia, blood viscosity, atherosclerosis, hypertension, etc. The number of people who die of cardiovascular and cerebrovascular diseases is as high as 15 million in the world every year.

Apixaban（Eliquis，BMS& Pfizer）

Apixaban is a new, oral, reversible, highly selective and direct factor Xa inhibitor for the prevention of venous thromboembolism (VTE) in adult patients undergoing elective hip or knee arthroplasty. It was approved by the European Union, Canada and FDA in 2012. It was officially launched in China in April 2013, and the sales situation continued to improve. In 2017, it became the best-selling cardiovascular drug, and the sales volume in 2019 reached 12.149 billion.

Atorvastatin（Lipitor，Pfizer）

Atorvastatin is used in patients with hypercholesterolemia. In 1997, it was first approved for use in the UK, and then listed in the United States. By 2017, the total sales of the drug in the world was nearly 140 billion US dollars, which was the first blockbuster drug in the history of medicine with total sales exceeding 100 billion US dollars. As the compound patent of the drug expired in 2011, a large number of generic drugs came into the market, which affected the market share of the original research products. But it still accounts for 2.096 billion of annual sales share in 2019.

  1. Infectious diseases

Among the 16 drug categories, 10 are AIDS drugs and 2 are hepatitis C drugs.

Biktarvy（BIC/FTC/TAF, Gilead）

Biktarvy was approved in the United States in February 2018, in the European Union in June, and launched in China in August 2019 as a complete program for the treatment of HIV-1 infected adults. Biktarvy has a very high virological inhibition rate, and the incidence of drug resistance and withdrawal adverse events is low.

Genvoya（E/C/F/TAF, Gilead）

Genvoya is China's first approved single tablet (STR) regimen based on TAF / FTC as the backbone, including integrase inhibitors, for the treatment of newly diagnosed adult HIV patients. It was launched in the United States and Europe at the end of 2015, and was approved in China in 2018. It is composed of antiviral drugs such as elvitegravir, emtricitabine, synergist cobicistat and tenofovir alfenamide (TAF). One tablet a day can play a very good anti HIV effect.
