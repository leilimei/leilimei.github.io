After HIV infection... It's useless to wait, stop and treat it actively!

2020-03-25

Today is the first day of the 21st century. I believe many people know what day it is. Because of the fear it brings to people, people have to remember it - HIV. The harm it brings to us is devastating. So in order to improve people's awareness of AIDS, the World Health Organization has designated December 1 as the World AIDS Day every year.

This year, the theme of World AIDS Day is "active detection, prevention and sharing of health".

HIV, also known as AIDS, originated in Africa. Since its discovery on June 5, 1981, it has become one of the biggest infectious diseases that threaten human health since the 21st century. Because of its long viral characteristics and incubation period, it was basically incurable before, and because its transmission route mainly depends on mother and child, saliva and sexual transmission, the first impression of people suffering from HIV is also Not clean, so for many patients suffering from HIV is very difficult to speak, so many patients are very negative, not willing to cooperate with the treatment, which is the main reason for the high death rate of AIDS.

According to the incomplete statistics of the global AIDS report, the number of AIDS patients in the world has reached 36 million, and about 5 million new AIDS patients are added every year. According to this momentum, if we do not prevent and control it, we can't believe what the world will be like 10 or 20 years later, which is really unthinkable.

China is also a country with a large number of people infected with AIDS. In recent years, the number of people suffering from AIDS is also growing rapidly, which greatly threatens the health of our people.

Over the years, through the unremitting efforts of experts from various countries, anti HIV drugs have also been developed to give hope to AIDS patients, so the attitude of patients has gradually changed from negative to positive treatment. At present, there are more than 30 kinds of anti HIV drugs on the market in the world. The categories include nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors, non nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors, protease inhibitors, fusion inhibitors, integrase inhibitors and CCR5 receptor inhibitors. These drugs can effectively inhibit and block the incidence rate of AIDS, but because of the specificity of AIDS, and after suppression and blocking, there is a high probability of recurrence. So the drug control rate of HIV is still very low.
