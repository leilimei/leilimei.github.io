Differences among amino acids, essential amino acids and nonessential amino acids

2020-04-24

Summary:
Amino acids are the material basis of human life metabolism and the basic unit of protein. The production, existence and extinction of life are all related to protein. If there is a lack of protein in the human body, the constitution of the light person will decline, the development will be slow, the resistance will be weakened, the anemia will be weak, the heavy person will form edema, even life-threatening.

Eight of the 20 amino acids that make up human protein are "essential amino acids" which can not be synthesized by human body or can not meet the needs of human body. If the human body lacks any kind of essential amino acids, it can lead to physiological dysfunction, affect the normal metabolism of the body, and finally lead to disease.

"Nonessential amino acids" cannot be misunderstood as nonessential, but they can be transformed from other amino acids in the body, not necessarily from food supply. If the human body lacks some nonessential amino acids, it will produce metabolic disorders.

Only when the ratio of "essential" and "nonessential" amino acids is appropriate can the utilization rate of amino acids be increased.

When the human body lacks amino acids, there will be problems:

  1. Influence on brain tissue of central nervous system: easily lead to Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease;

  2. The effect on cardiovascular system: the elasticity of blood vessel declines, leading to atherosclerosis;

  3. Effect on liver: fatty liver is easy to form;

  4. Influence on kidney: lead to low function of kidney;

  5. Influence on gastrointestinal system: forming digestive and absorption dysfunction;

  6. The effects on the motor system are as follows: muscle fiber atrophy, energy supply decline, limb weakness;

  7. The influence on the immune mechanism of human body: once the protein is insufficient, the resistance of the elderly will decrease, and various diseases will easily occur;

  8. When the supply of amino acids is insufficient, that is to say, the raw materials for the synthesis of antioxidant and antioxidant are insufficient. The accumulation of free radicals and acid substances will accelerate the aging of human body. If there are too many free radicals, the small ones will be fatigue, the large ones will be aging.

In a word, amino acids are the raw materials for the synthesis of human protein, hormone, enzyme and antibody. With the improvement of people's health care and nutrition awareness, the need for amino acid nutrition must be gradually increased.
