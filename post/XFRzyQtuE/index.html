Comparison of advantages and disadvantages of protein purification methods

2020-04-29

  2. Replacement of buffer

  3. Ion exchange chromatography

  4. Affinity chromatography

  5. Hydrophobic action

  6. Exclusion chromatography

  7. Electrophoresis

1、Protein precipitation

Protein is soluble in water because of hydrophilic amino acids on its surface. If the ionic strength of the solution is especially high or very low at the isoelectric point of the protein, the protein tends to separate out from the solution. Ammonium sulfate is the most commonly used salt to precipitate protein, because it has good solubility in cold buffer, which is conducive to maintaining the activity of the target protein. Ammonium sulfate fractionation is often used as the first step of protein purification in the laboratory.

It can preliminarily extract protein and remove non protein components. The protein is stable in ammonium sulfate precipitation, and intermediate products can be preserved in this state for a short time. At present, this method is mostly used for protein purification. There are still some problems in the large-scale production of ammonium sulfate precipitation method, which is very corrosive to stainless steel appliances. Other salts such as sodium sulfate do not have this problem, but their purification effect is not as good as ammonium sulfate. In addition to salting out, the protein can also be precipitated with polymers such as peg and antifreeze.

Peg is an inert substance, which has the same stable effect as ammonium sulfate on the protein. Under slow stirring, the PEG concentration in the cold protein solution is gradually increased. The protein precipitation can be obtained by centrifugation or filtration, and the protein can be preserved in this state for a long time without damage. Protein precipitation is not a good method for protein purification, because it can only achieve several times of purification effect, and we need thousands of times of purification before we achieve the goal. Its advantage is that it can free the protein from the medium and cell lysate mixed with protease and other harmful impurities.

  1. Replacement of buffer

Although the replacement of buffer can not improve the purity of protein, it plays an important role in protein purification. Different methods of protein purification need different pH and ion strength buffers. If you use ammonium sulfate to precipitate the protein, there is no doubt that the protein is in a high salt environment. You need to find a way to desalinate. The available methods are to use semi permeable membrane dialysis, and to remove salt by frequently changing dialysis liquid.

This method is OK, but it takes several hours, usually overnight, and it is difficult to use in large-scale purification. In the new equipment, the dialysis membrane is sandwiched between two plates, one side of the plate is added with buffer solution, the other side is added with protein solution to be desalted, and one side of the protein solution is pressurized by pump, so that the solution on both sides can reach balance within a few hours.

If the pressure on the protein solution is increased, more water and salt can be forced to enter the dialysis solution through the dialysis membrane to achieve the purpose of protein concentration. There are also desalting columns for sale. The packing in the column is particles with small pore size. Protein molecules cannot enter the pore, and flow out of the column prior to high concentration salt ions, so as to separate them.

Each step of protein purification will result in the loss of the target protein, especially the step of buffer equilibrium. The protein will bind to any surface that it can touch, and the rapid changes in shear force, foaming and ionic strength will easily deactivate the protein.

  1. Ion exchange chromatography

This is the most effective method among all protein purification and concentration methods. Based on the interaction between protein and ion exchange resin, the same protein can be combined with either anion exchange resin (which can combine negatively charged molecules) or cation exchange resin by selecting different buffers.

There are four kinds of charged groups used in the resin: diethylaminoethyl for weak anion exchange resin; carboxymethyl for weak cation exchange resin; quaternary ammonium for strong anion exchange resin; methyl sulfonate for strong cation exchange resin. Protein is composed of amino acids, which have different total charge in different pH environment. Most proteins are negatively charged at physiological pH (PH6-8) and need to be purified by anion exchange column. At extreme pH, protein denaturation and inactivation should be avoided as much as possible.

Due to the different charge number of different protein under a specific pH, the binding force with resin is also different. With the increase of salt concentration in buffer or the change of pH, the protein is eluted in turn according to the strength of binding force. In industrial production, it is more to change the salt concentration than to change the pH value, because the former is easier to control. In the laboratory, almost always use the gradient of salt concentration to elute the ion-exchange column.

With the help of the pump, the salt concentration in the buffer solution flowing into the column can rise steadily. When the ion strength can neutralize the charge of the protein, the protein will be eluted from the column. However, it is difficult to control the salt concentration accurately in industrial production, so it is often used to elute step by step, but not to continuously increase the salt gradient.

Compared with the exclusion chromatography, the ion exchange specificity is better, more parameters can be adjusted to obtain the best purification effect, and the resin is cheaper. It is worth mentioning that even under the most precisely controlled conditions, pure proteins can not be obtained only by ion-exchange method, and other purification steps are needed.

  1. Affinity chromatography

Affinity chromatography is based on the specific binding of the target protein to the solid-phase ligand, and other heteroproteins flow through the column. The problems of this method are: the monoclonal antibody is very expensive and needs to be purified first; the binding force between the monoclonal antibody and the target protein is too strong.

It needs to be eluted under harsh conditions, which will inactivate the target protein and destroy the monoclonal antibody; other proteins in the mixture, such as protease, may also destroy the antibody or non-specific binding with them; some monoclonal antibodies will also be separated from the resin and mixed into the production during the purification process It also needs to be removed from the end products.

The affinity column is usually used in the later stage of purification, when the sample volume has been reduced and most impurities have been removed. Glutathione S-transferase S-transferase (GST) is one of the most commonly used affinity chromatography purification tags. The recombinant protein with this tag can be purified by cross-linked glutathione chromatography medium, but this method has the following disadvantages: first, the GST on the protein must be folded properly to form a spatial structure combined with glutathione in order to be purified by this method;

second, the GST tag has up to 220 amino acids, so large It may affect the solubility of the expressed protein and form inclusion bodies, which will destroy the natural structure of the protein and make it difficult to carry out structural analysis. Sometimes, even after purification, enzyme digestion to remove GST tags may not solve the problem. Another applicable affinity purification label is 6-histidine label.

The imidazole side chain of histidine is compatible with and binds to metal ions such as nickel, zinc and cobalt. The target protein with histidine label combines with nickel column under neutral and weak alkaline conditions, and elutes with imidazole competitively at low pH. Histidine labeling has many advantages compared with GST. First, because there are only six amino acids and the molecular weight is very small, it needs to be removed by enzyme cutting. Second, the protein can be purified under denaturation conditions, and the binding force can still be maintained in high concentration of urea and guanidine.

In addition, 6 histidine labeling has no immunogenicity, and the recombinant protein can be directly used for injection into animals, and does not affect the immunological analysis. Although there are so many advantages, the label still has some disadvantages, such as the inclusion body of the target protein is easy to form, difficult to dissolve, poor stability and wrong folding.

When the nickel column is purified, the metal nickel ion is easy to fall off and leak out into the protein solution, which will not only destroy the amino acid side chain of the target protein through oxidation, but also affect the purification effect by nonspecific adsorption of protein on the column. If the target protein can specifically bind with a carbohydrate, or need a special cofactor, the carbohydrate or cofactor can be immobilized into an affinity column. After binding, the target protein can be eluted with a high concentration of carbohydrate or cofactor.

  1. Hydrophobic action

The chromatography protein is composed of hydrophobic and hydrophilic amino acids. Hydrophobic amino acids are located in the center of protein spatial structure and far away from the surface water molecules. The hydrophilic amino acid residues were located on the protein surface.

Because hydrophilic amino acids attract many water molecules, usually the whole protein molecule is surrounded by water molecules, and hydrophobic amino acids will not be exposed. In the high salt concentration environment, the hydrophobic region of the protein will be exposed and combined with the hydrophobic ligand on the surface of the hydrophobic medium. Different proteins have different hydrophobicity and different hydrophobic force.

By gradually reducing the salt concentration in the buffer solution to wash the column, when the salt concentration is very low, the protein returns to its natural state, and the hydrophobic force weakened is eluted.

The selectivity of hydrophobic resin is determined by the structure of hydrophobic ligands. The common straight chain ligands are alkyl ligands and aryl ligands. The longer the chain, the stronger the ability of binding proteins. The choice of the ideal resin type should be based on the chemical properties of the target protein.

The resin with too strong binding force cannot be selected. The resin with too strong binding force will be difficult to elute. Therefore, the phenyl resin with medium binding force should be selected at the beginning to explore the conditions.

In order to make it easier to choose the right medium, Amersham Biosciences launched a hydrophobic resin selection kit, which includes five different resins for comparison. Hydrophobic chromatography is very suitable for the next step of ion exchange purification, because hydrophobic interaction chromatography samples at high salt concentration, the products obtained from ion exchange can be used without changing buffer.

The protein is eluted in the low salt buffer, and the next step of replacing the buffer before purification is omitted, which not only saves time, but also reduces the loss of protein.

6.Exclusion chromatography

Also known as gel filtration or molecular sieves. The packed particles in the column are porous media, and the amount of liquid that can be contained around the particles in the column is called mobile phase, also called invalid volume.

Too large protein can not enter the pore of particles, only exist in the solution of invalid volume, and will be eluted from the column as early as possible, which has no purification effect on this part of protein. Due to the different molecular sizes of various proteins, the ability of diffusion into particles with specific pore size is also different. Large protein molecules will be eluted first. The smaller the molecule, the later it will be eluted.

In order to obtain the best purification effect, the pore size should be chosen to elute the target protein near the midpoint of the invalid volume and the total column bed volume. Exclusion chromatography has advantages that other methods do not have. Firstly, it can purify protein with a wide molecular weight range. TOSOH biosep's polymer resin has a rejection limit of up to 200000 KD. Secondly, the shape of resin micropores is suitable for separating spherical proteins, and there is no need for organic solvents that can cause protein denaturation in the purification process. It should be noted that some proteins are not suitable for purification by gel filtration, because the resins used in this technology are mild hydrophilic, and proteins with high charge density are easily adsorbed on them.

Exclusion chromatography has never been used in the early stage of purification process, because this method requires high concentration of samples, the sample loading can only be between 1% and 4% of the column volume, the column should be thin and long to get good separation effect, the resin itself is relatively expensive, and the large-scale industrial production is not suitable.

  1. Electrophoresis

Acrylamide gel electrophoresis is usually used to examine the complexity of protein mixture samples and monitor the purification effect. However, it is difficult to scale up to the scale of preparation without losing the accuracy. With the increase of gel thickness, the thermal effect of electrophoresis will seriously interfere with the swimming of protein. In basic research, sometimes only a small amount of pure protein is needed for research, such as protein sequencing.

At this time, electrophoretic purification is a simple and fast method. Acrylamide gel electrophoresis is also an important analytical tool in the process of protein purification. It can detect which gradient ion exchange column salt eluent is in the target protein. It can be used to judge that with the rapid development of various disciplines in recent years, the demand for protein purification technology has been increasing. The existing purification methods have been improved and new purification methods have emerged one after another. Hydroxyapatite is the crystal of calcium phosphate.

Because of its unstable physical and chemical properties and poor binding ability, it is difficult to be used in chromatography. In recent years, bio rad company has improved the ratio of calcium and phosphorus to form spherical, porous and stable ceramic hydroxyapatite particles. Its positively charged calcium ion and negatively charged phosphate ion can be combined with the carboxyl and amino groups of protein respectively.

By adjusting the pH value of the buffer, the acid and basic amino acids can selectively combine with the resin, and the protein can be separated by changing the salt concentration of the buffer. The results show that the two proteins with the same isoelectric point, molecular weight and hydrophobicity can be separated by this method.
