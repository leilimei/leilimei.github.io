Determination of amphoteric properties and isoelectric point of proteins

2020-05-28

Key words: amphoteric properties of protein

Key words: amphoteric properties of protein, isoelectric point, amphoteric electrolyte, charged group, phenol mercaptoguanidine imidazole group, amphoteric reaction

Experimental principle

Proteins are amphoteric electrolytes. In addition to the N-terminal α - amino group and C-terminal α - carboxyl group, there are also side chain groups of some amino acid residues on the peptide chain, such as phenol group, mercapto group, guanidine group, imidazole group and other groups, which can be dissociated into charged groups. Therefore, there is the following balance in the protein solution:

Cationic amphoteric anion

pH < pIpH = pIpH > pI

In the electric field: move to the cathode without moving to the anode to adjust the pH of the solution so that the acid dissociation and alkaline dissociation of the protein molecules are equal, that is, the positive and negative charges are equal, and the net charges are zero. At this time, the pH value of the solution is called the isoelectric point of the protein. At the isoelectric point, the protein solubility is the smallest and the turbidity of the solution is the largest. The isoelectric point of the protein can be determined by preparing different pH buffers and observing the solubility of the protein in these buffers.

Reagents and equipment
1、 Reagents

1mol / L acetic acid: suck 99.5% acetic acid (specific gravity 1.05) 2.875ml, and add water to 50ml.

0.1mol/l acetic acid: suck 5ml of 1mol / L acetic acid and add water to 50ml.

0.01mol/l acetic acid: suck 5ml of 0.1mol/l acetic acid and add water to 50ml.

0.2mol/l NaOH: weigh the naoh2.000g, add water to 50ml, and prepare 1mol / L NaOH. Then measure 10ml of 1mol / L NaOH, add water to 50ml, and prepare 0.2mol/l NaOH.

0.2mol/l HCl: suck 37.2% (specific gravity 1.19) HCl 4.17ml, add water to 50ml, prepare 1mol / L HCl. Then draw 10ml of 1mol / L HCl, add water to 50ml, and prepare 0.2mol/l HCl.

0.01% bromocresol green indicator: weigh 0.005g bromocresol green, add 0.29ml 1mol / L NaOH, and then add water to 50ml.

2、 Test sample

0.5% casein solution: weigh 0.25g casein into a 50ml volumetric flask, add about 20ml of water, add 5ml of 1mol / L NaOH accurately, add 5ml of 1mol / L acetic acid accurately after the casein dissolves, and finally add water to dilute to 50ml, shake well.

3、 Equipment

Tube 1.5 × 15cm (× 8); pipette 1ml (× 4), 2ml (× 4), 10ml (× 2); rubber head dropper (× 2).

Operation method

1、 Amphoteric reaction of proteins

(1) Take a test tube, add 0.5% casein 1ml, add 4 drops of bromocresol green indicator, and shake well. At this time, the solution is blue without precipitation.

(2) Slowly add 0.2mol/l HCl into the rubber head dropper, and shake while adding until a large amount of precipitation is formed. The pH value of the solution is close to the isoelectric point of casein. Observe the change of solution color.

(3) If 0.2mol/l HCl is added continuously, the precipitation will gradually decrease and disappear. Observe the change of solution color at this time.

(4) Add 0.2mol/l NaOH dropwise for neutralization, and the precipitation appears again. After adding 0.2mol/l NaOH, the precipitation disappeared. Observe the change of solution color.

2、 Determination of isoelectric point of casein

(1) Take 7 tubes of the same specification, and add the following reagents accurately according to the following table:

(3) The symbols -, +, + + +, + + + + are used to represent the turbidity of each tube. The isoelectric point of casein was determined by turbidity. The pH value of the most turbid tube is the isoelectric point of casein.

matters needing attention

In the experiment of measuring the isoelectric point, it is required that the concentration and amount of various reagents are quite accurate.
