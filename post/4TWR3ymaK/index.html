1、 Overview of phage display technology

Phage display technology was developed in the mid-1980s. Smith G first described the principle of displaying foreign peptide fragments on the surface of filamentous phage. In this method, a large number of polypeptide coding sequences are introduced into the capsid protein gene of human phage by DNA recombination technology, so that all kinds of expressed polypeptide will appear on the surface of phage in the form of fusion with PIII or pVIII protein, that is, to construct phage polypeptide display library first. In the past 10 years, great progress has been made in the construction of phage display peptide library and the screening of multi defense coding sequence combined with known target substances from the library by biopanning.

In recent years, in the identification of small peptide sequence of protein or receptor, in the study of peptide mimic of known protein in structure and function, in the screening and identification of peptides that can combine with biological target systems such as human virus, cell, tissue and tumor, the research is increasing. Phage peptide display library provides the possibility to isolate a peptide which can specifically bind to important proteins such as antibodies related to inflammation and integrin that mediates cell adhesion.

In particular, it is possible to find some multiligands that lead to the production of peptide mimicry drugs. Phages and libraries are widely used. They can display not only small peptides, but also large proteins, such as scFvs.

Filamentous phages propagate in pili positive bacteria, which do not cause bacterial lysis, but can make bacteria secrete multiple copies, and display the insertion sequence of phages on its surface. Phages that bind to a target molecule can be eluted and then grow and amplify in bacteria. This is the so-called biopanning, which can enrich and bind related phages on the target molecules after repeated times.

The binding peptide sequence can be determined by the nucleotide sequence of the phage genome part encoding the peptide. The final inserted peptide sequence can be reconstructed by recombinant technology or synthetic method, through which specific and selective ligands of the target receptor can be found.

In the display library, the multi defense sequence is fused in or near the amino terminal of the smallest capsid protein III. Another fusion target that has been used to a lesser extent is large capsid protein VIII. The advantage of using PII is that the insertion of considerable peptides or proteins does not result in the loss of phage infectivity.

Only about five copies of PII were anchored at the top of each phage particle. For example, when using fuse 5 vector, there is an insertion sequence on each PII, so it is a multivalent display of polypeptide. It has been reported in the past few years that multivalent display is not a necessary condition for the application of peptide ligands of different target molecules discovered by this carrier in vivo and in vitro.

Phage particles are surprisingly resistant to harsh environmental conditions, such as at pH 2.2 and 4-6m.

Urea is used to depolymerize the binding of target molecules to phages without losing the ability to infect bacteria. It should be noted that the phage bound to the target does not need to be eluted from the 64 well plate or tissue. Usually, the bacteria can be directly added to the 64 well plate or homogenate tissue, and the relevant phage can be obtained after being poled.

If the biochemical details of receptor ligand interaction are known, more specific methods can also be used for elution. For example, peptides containing arginine glycine aspartic acid (RGD) and cationic chelators such as EDTA are used to dissociate phages bound to α 5 β 1.

2、 Proteins and receptors that recognize short peptide sequences

Once a protein can be purified or expressed on the cell surface after transfection, the peptide library provides a possibility to qualitatively and specifically bind to the ligand on that protein. In living cells, protein protein reactions are often induced by a large surface area, but some are only related to the binding of a small peptide. The phage display peptide library is particularly suitable for intermolecular reactions under the guidance of the research team. The most important application of phage peptide display library is to determine the antigenic determinant of antibody.

Antibody recognition of small peptides is usually based on only 3-4 conserved amino acids. It is possible for phage display to reveal polypeptides and delineate protein regions recognized by antibodies. The determination of the determinants of antibodies involved in autoimmune disorders may give important information to the immune mechanism of the disease.

A peptide displayed by a phage can also be reversely applied to produce an antibody against the displayed peptide. Filamentous phage is a very strong immunogen, by which we can separate a special peptide antibody displayed by phage.

There are some molecules in the immune system that can recognize small peptides. The main function of histocompatibility molecule (MHC) is to recognize the proteins derived from endogenous and exogenous sources on the host cell surface. When a cell becomes abnormal or invades the host by harmful microorganisms, the immune system can recognize it based on the sequence of peptides that will coalesce to the MHC molecule. Phage display library has been used to describe the peptide structure recognized by each MHC molecule.

Cell surface integrin family can recognize RGD. These whole proteins mediate cell adhesion to a variety of extracellular matrix proteins containing RGD, such as fibronectin, vitronectin and fibyiflogen. The screening of phage display library has proved that each of integrin α 5 β 1, α lib, β 3, α V β 3, α V β 5 has a slightly different peptide specific binding, and is willing to bind RGD Peptides in a unique cyclic disulfide bond sequence.

This reflects the different preference of each integrin for different exoproteins. Filtered combinations have been found. The peptide of α 5 β 1 contains crgdgwc sequence, while the binding peptide of α V β 5 has a more complex structure, cdcrgdcfc (also known as rgd-4c). The binding peptide of the integrin contains four semi deaminases. The pairing of these semi deaminases can form a mixture of different disulfide bonds. The activity of the dicyclic polypeptides synthesized by the random oxidation of cysteine is enough to cause cell adhesion. Later experiments proved that another arrangement of secondary bonds can give more active polypeptides.

Phage display of peptides often reveals new peptide ligands that were not expected. A circular polypeptide, creptawac, can be screened out by using α 5 β 1 integrin, which in turn is a specific ligand of this integrin, and does not bind to other members of the integrin family. This polypeptide can prevent α 5 β 1 mediated cell adhesion to fibronectin. Another integrin binds to NgR, almost the reverse order of RGD. The NgR sequence appears in many protein sequences, such as fibronectin, but it has a relatively low affinity for integrin. The role of NgR in the reaction of tomb protein and integrin is not clear.

Phage display libraries are also commonly used to select ligands that do not recognize RGD or NgR, but that recognize integrins of other matrix proteins, such as α 6 β 1, a membrane protein, and α m β 2, a leukocyte receptor for immunoglobulin like mucins and proteins.

The ligands that can bind to SRC homologous region III (SH3) in one of two pseudosymmetrical ways I and II have also been studied by phage display. SH3 exists in many protein kinases and binds to proline rich regions of signal molecules. The conservative order of type I is rpllpplp, while type II is opposite rib applppr.

The proline rich region in the protein reacts with the kinase and is involved in signal transduction. The peptide library is also used to discover the SHZ ligands in the common domain of another protein kinase and signal transduction protein. SHZ region recognizes a peptide containing phosphorylated tyrosine.

3、 New peptide mimics and minimization proteins

The antibody does not always correspond to the linear amino acid sequence in a protein, but can recognize the special configuration of a discontinuous determinant cluster & 0 & 0; folded protein. To screen a phage library with some antibody, the peptide produced will simulate the structure of the folded protein.

These ligands are called mimotopes. If cysteine is blocked, the peptide will be inactivated, because it will not be folded into a proper configuration. In addition to antibodies, mimic clusters or structural mimics of molecules that react with several proteins have been found by phage display (see Table 2, omitted).

A small peptide with appropriate configuration can replace the function of natural protein. Random phage display of multi defense library, combined with some other methods, can provide a very good program to study the effective mimic of protein and the smallest peptide sequence with activity.

For example, the mimic polypeptides of peptide hormone EPO and TPO have a good therapeutic effect on human diseases. The dimer of 14 amino acid circular TPO mimic polypeptides has the activity of 332 amino acid TPO, but only 13 The simulated ligand of amino acids can simulate the properties of 162 amino acids EPO.

4、 Selection of peptides for binding to complex biological systems

In recent years, phage peptide libraries have been very useful in selecting peptides that can bind to more complex targets. These targets are human viruses, living cells, mouse tissues and tumors (see Table 3, omitted). When these complex target systems are applied, it is necessary to have a guided phage screening program to promote the specific peptide mediated binding far higher than the adhesion of the background phage.

The first example of application is the separation of peptide ligands of urokinase receptor by cells transfected with urokinase receptor. Two different cell lines infected with urokinase receptor, such as a mouse and an insect cell line, were used to screen peptide ligands. At this time, only urokinase receptor is common between the two cells, and the motif of the separated peptide is not similar to the known urokinase binding to the receptor. This screening system based on the expression of CO receptor on cell surface is also used to screen the ligand of melanin receptor.

Another way to elute phages that specifically bind is with reagents that interfere with the interaction, such as antibodies and peptides. Human platelets have been used as targets to screen peptide ligands of thrombin receptor. Phages bound to platelets are eluted with a known peptide ligand.

Antibody elution has been used to identify binding peptides of different capsid proteins of human adenovirus. The characterization of the polypeptides that react with the virus will provide clues for the proteins that bind to the virus and the potential receptors of the virus distributed on the cell surface. For example, the sequence of some peptides isolated is similar to that of known adenovirus receptors, one of which is integrin.

The application of peptides combined with virus will interfere with virus infection and prevent virus combination and internalization. That polypeptide, clrsgrc, was screened by the display library with the corresponding virus. It is worth noting that the purified phage particles which display the peptide of the virus can also prevent the infection of the virus.

Peptides can bind to the surface of cells. If the target receptor is unknown, the peptides that recognize special cells can also be isolated.

More complicated is in vivo screening. When the phage library is injected intravenously, it can be found that the multi prevention sequence is located in one organ of the mouse. After circulating in vivo for a few minutes and then perfusion with phosphate, the phage targeted at endothelium can be obtained (see Table 3). Recently, the receptor of polypeptide located in lung has been identified. The discovery of polypeptides in tumor vascular cells will be beneficial to the study of tumor inhibition.

Therefore, it is of great theoretical significance and potential application value to use the phage display method to find the mimic of the ligand of the known receptor for the development of class I new biotechnological drugs with independent intellectual property rights for disease treatment. At the same time, the tissue and organ specificity of peptide sequence was found after the phage peptide display library was injected in vivo. For example, the sequence of amino acids distributed in mouse brain provided a meaningful way for cssrldac to enter specific target cells and tissues.

With the aging society in China, the treatment of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease and Parkinson's disease is of great significance. However, in the treatment of central nervous system diseases with neurotrophic factors, due to the existence of blood-brain barrier, peptide drugs can not enter the brain effectively in other ways except intraventricular injection. Therefore, how to properly solve the way of administration of peptide drugs has always been the focus of pharmacists.

Blood brain barrier is caused by the tight arrangement of brain vascular endothelial cells and the lack of endocytosis. Of course, it is also related to the lipid solubility, polarity, molecular weight (no more than 400d-600d) and molecular shape of drug molecules. In order to break through the problem of blood-brain barrier, a lot of work has been reported, such as chemical modification of drugs, making hydrophobic homologues and connecting the active components to some special carriers such as insulin receptor and transferrin receptor. Methods such as carotid injection of hypertonic mannitol or bradykinin can also be used to open the blood-brain barrier at one time.

The application of activators and Chokers of P-gp will help to increase the concentration of central nervous drugs in the brain and reduce the side effects of peripheral nerve drugs on the central nervous system. Studies have shown that intraperitoneal injection of PNA results in the distribution of PNA in the brain. The fusion of 120K protein with human HIV Tat protein results in the distribution of the protein in various tissues including brain. Or the peptide can be connected to the monoclonal antibody (mAb) of OX26.

All these methods are lack of CO augmentation and are not perfect. People are eager to have better methods to break the blood-brain barrier. We hope to find out alternative vascular endothelial marker polypeptide molecules through phage display library work. Among them, there will be analog ligand sequence of known receptors (insulin receptor, transferrin receptor) and unknown polypeptide sequence related to transport system. We will determine those polypeptide sequences with permeable blood-brain barrier.

Their clinical application is limited by the blood-brain barrier.

All of these members interact with two kinds of receptors: the low affinity receptor p75 (p75NGFR, p75NTR) of NGF and the Trk receptor family that can bind different neurotrophic factors with high affinity.
