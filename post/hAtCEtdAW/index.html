What are the uses of plant polypeptides?

2020-01-19 3 min read # Peptide industry news

Polypeptide is a kind of compound whose molecular structure is between amino acid and protein. It is composed of amino acids, but different from protein, it belongs to the intermediate substance between them. A compound in which amino acids can be linked to each other by peptide bonds is called a peptide. Polypeptides are divided into plant polypeptides and animal polypeptides. Next, I will introduce the function of plant polypeptides.

The role of plant polypeptides:

Polypeptides play an important and irreplaceable regulatory role in human body, which involves almost all physiological activities of human body. Such as: nerve, digestion, absorption, metabolism, circulation, growth, reproduction, endocrine, etc. At present, many peptides have been used in the development of medicine and health food. Insulin, which we are familiar with, is a polypeptide. Oxytocin and vasopressin are polypeptide hormones. In the society of high rhythm and high tension, people with abnormal sleep are expanding. Hypnopeptide is a good choice because it has no side effects. Other effective drugs for diabetes, gastric ulcer, pancreatitis, dwarfism and gallbladder contraction are all peptides. For the human immune system, polypeptides are more important and direct, such as mannan peptide, thymosin, albumin polypeptides, etc., which can activate macrophages and T cells rapidly, so as to improve the immune level of human cells and prevent the infection caused by coronavirus.
