【1】 Nat Neurosci: how does a high salt diet affect the brain?

DOI：10.1038/s1593-017-0059-z

A recent study shows that high salt diet not only brings risks such as high blood pressure, but also has a negative impact on the brain, which in turn affects the level of intellectual performance.

This latest study found that after the mice received a high salt diet, the level of cerebral blood flow decreased significantly, the integrity of cerebral blood vessels was affected, and the cognitive function performance of mice was affected.

However, the researchers found that these effects are not secondary effects of high blood pressure. In fact, they are mediated by signals from the gut to the brain.

The study, conducted by researchers from Weill Cornell Medical School in New York, was published in the latest issue of nature neuroscience.

【2】 Do you have diabetes if you eat too much salt?

Increasing salt intake TID to diabetes risk

According to a recent study, excessive salt intake may also increase the risk of diabetes in adults.

The study included data from thousands of volunteers in Sweden. The results showed that for adults, the risk of type II diabetes increased by 65% for every 2.5G (equivalent to half a teaspoon of salt) added per day when salt intake exceeded the normal range.

In addition, the researchers found that people with higher daily salt intake (1.25 teaspoons or more) had a 72% increased risk of type II diabetes compared to people with lower daily salt intake.

The study was led by bahareh rasouli of environmental medicine branch of Karolinska Institute in Stockholm, Sweden. The results were presented at the recent annual meeting of the Association for the study of diabetes (EASD).

【3】 A 12-year study found that high salt intake doubled the risk of heart failure

According to a study of more than 4000 people from the ESC conference over 12 years, high salt diet doubled the risk of heart failure.

Pekka jousilahti, a professor at the National Institute of welfare and health in Finland, said: "a high salt diet is one of the main causes of hypertension, coronary heart disease (CHD) and stroke. Apart from CHD and stroke, heart failure is one of the most important cardiovascular diseases in the world, including Europe, and the mechanism of high salt intake on heart failure is not clear. "

This study evaluated the relationship between salt intake and heart failure. It needs some methods to estimate the salt intake of individuals. It is very difficult to find the right people. The study looked at 24-hour sodium excretion, which is believed to be the gold standard for estimating salt intake by individuals.

The population of this study is 4630 men and women randomly selected from 25 to 64 years old who participated in the North Karelia salt study and national finrisk study in Finland. Their basic data include: questionnaire response of healthy behavior, weight, height, blood pressure, laboratory analysis of venous blood sample and 24-hour urine sample collection. In the experiment place, the nurse will measure the urine volume of the subjects, and take out 100ml for sample analysis, 17.1mmol of sodium excretion can be converted into 1g of salt intake.

【4】 Nature: high salt intake will change the intestinal flora of mice

Intestinal flora is a normal microorganism in human intestine, such as Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus, which can synthesize many kinds of vitamins necessary for human growth and development, such as B vitamins (vitamin B1, B2, B6, B12), vitamin K, niacin, pantothenic acid, etc., and can also use protein residues to synthesize essential amino acids, such as aspartic acid, phenylalanine, valine and threonine, etc., and participate in carbohydrate and threonine Protein metabolism, but also promote the absorption of iron, magnesium, zinc and other mineral elements. These nutrients play an important role in human health, once lacking, it will cause a variety of diseases.

There are 10 trillion bacteria in human intestine, which can affect body weight and digestive capacity, resist the risk of infection and autoimmune diseases, and control the response of human body to cancer drugs.

On November 16, a study published online in nature said that high salt intake would change the intestinal flora of mice. In view of the increasing recognition of the role of intestinal microflora in diseases, the above findings highlight the potential of intestinal microflora as a potential therapeutic target against salt sensitive diseases.

【5】 A high salt or sugar diet can make you suffer from high blood pressure quickly

The down side of your sweet and salty addition could be rapid onset high blood pressure

Edible fructose is a kind of fruit derived sugar, which exists in many sugary drinks and processed foods. Fructose consumption in the United States and around the world is associated with many epidemics such as diabetes, obesity, metabolic syndrome and high blood pressure. A new study recently found that fructose is abundant in the American diet, which can prompt individuals to develop salt sensitive hypertension rapidly.

"Most American adults consume 10% or more of their total calories from the added sugar." Said Dr. Kevin Gordon. Because beverages are the most common source of added sugar in the diet of the United States, the researchers conducted an experiment in which rats were divided into two groups. The first group of rats fed drinking water containing 20% fructose to simulate excessive consumption of human soft drinks. Rats in the second group were fed with boiled water for two weeks. During the second week, the first group added extra salt to their diet.

"A specific combination of fructose and high salt was introduced into the first group of rats during the second week, which resulted in a rapid increase of blood pressure and the occurrence of hypertension. Fructose associated hypertension is associated with increased sodium retention, reduced sodium excretion, and * factors that help to clear excess salt in the body. We have observed that fructose related hypertension is only caused by a diet with fructose and high salt, which is not a normal salt diet. "

【6】 Cell Metab: Chinese scientists answer how sodium intake affects blood glucose balance

doi：10.1016/j.cmet.2016.02.019

Recently, researchers from the Third Military Medical University of China published a new research progress in the international academic journal cell metabolism. They found that the intake of sodium salt can regulate blood glucose balance through a signal pathway mediated by PPAR δ in adipose tissue. This study is of great significance to guide people's healthy diet and help diabetic patients prevent cardiovascular metabolic diseases.

High sodium intake is a major risk factor for hypertension in diabetic patients. Promoting the excretion of sodium can reduce the risk of cardiovascular metabolic disease in diabetic patients. However, the relationship between sodium intake and glucose balance is still not well understood.

In this latest study, researchers reported that high sodium intake significantly increased urinary sodium excretion in wild-type mice, but this effect was blocked in fat tissue-specific PPAR δ knockout mice and diabetic mice. At the same time, the researchers found that PPAR δ, which is activated by the use of agonists or high sodium salt, can regulate the level of adiponectin in adipose tissue, and then inhibit the function of renal sodium glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2). SGLT2 is a major transport protein for renal glucose reabsorption, and SGLT2 inhibitor is an important diabetes drug.

【7】 Jncs: high salt diet can also promote obesity

Salt could lead to weight gain by driving pretty food intake

Too much salt is harmful to the body, which is a common sense of life. Excessive salt intake will increase blood pressure, increase the risk of heart disease and so on. But two new studies have recently found that a high salt diet can have another dire effect - it can lead to excessive consumption of fatty foods, which increases the risk of diseases such as obesity.

Both studies were conducted by Russell keast and his colleagues from the University of Addison, Italy. In an article published in Journal of nutrition and chemical senses, they called on the food industry to minimize the use of salt or sodium in food production.

Prior to this, Professor keast mentioned in their early study that those who were more sensitive to the taste of fat were more likely to eat more high-fat foods, thus increasing the risk of obesity.

Their latest research is also based on the above results, finding that salt content in some foods may affect how much we eat.

【8】 Cell research: molecular evidence that high salt diet leads to cell inflammation

doi：10.1038/cr.2015.87

Long term high salt diet may induce some chronic diseases, such as cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes and autoimmune disease. These diseases are closely related to the functional defects of the immune system. However, how does high salt diet affect the immune system and induce these diseases? A research group led by Duan Shengzhong of the Institute of nutrition research, Chinese Academy of Sciences, found that high salt environment would lead to the increase of inflammatory factors in macrophages, while the decrease of anti-inflammatory factors and endocytosis related factors in cells. This study may provide a basis for the explanation of diseases related to high salt diet. The study was published in cell research.

In order to find out the relationship between high salt environment and immune system function, the research team led by Professor Duan first detected the expression of inflammation related genes in monocyte macrophages in high salt environment. They found that the expression of inflammation related genes (such as CCL1) increased while that of anti-inflammatory genes (such as CR1) decreased. They also found that the immune activation signaling pathway induced by high salt environment was partially overlapped with that induced by lipopolysaccharide (LPS). P38 / CFOs and ERK1 / 2 / STAT6 are in the same part of two immune activation pathways.

【9】 JCEM: high salt diet may increase the risk of cardiovascular disease in diabetic individuals

doi：10.1210/jc.2013-4315

Recently, in a research paper published in the International Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & metabolism, researchers from Niigata University in Japan said that the risk of cardiovascular disease in diabetic patients with high salt diet was twice that of those with low salt diet.

According to the CDC data of the United States, about 29.1 million Americans have different forms of diabetes, and these people are also among the people at risk of heart disease. Between 2003 and 2006, the death rate of diabetic patients with cardiovascular disease was 1.7 times higher than that of non diabetic patients.

Researcher Chika Dr. Horikawa said that this study provides clear scientific evidence for us to reveal that low salt diet reduces the risk of cardiovascular disease in diabetic patients. Although many guiding principles suggest that diabetic patients should reduce salt intake to avoid complications, this study is the first to clarify the benefits and molecular mechanism of low salt diet for diabetic patients.

In this paper, the researchers studied diabetes patients aged between 40 and 70. At the same time, the researchers conducted a survey of 1588 individuals on their diet, including the intake of salt in their diet. The researchers analyzed the data of cardiovascular disease complications of these participants in the past eight years.

  1. Nature: scientists have found that high salt affects immune system and increases the incidence rate of multiple sclerosis.

doi：10.1038/nature11984

Recently published in nature, it is suggested that the amount of salt in the diet may induce the immune system to fight against the body, leading to immune system diseases such as multiple sclerosis (MS).

Although there is no treatment for MS, the researchers say the findings are interesting and believable.

The wrong attack of the immune system will lead to autoimmune diseases such as type 1 diabetes, rheumatoid and multiple sclerosis.

Gene mutation will increase the risk of autoimmune disease, and environmental factors also have an important impact on the disease. An important cause of multiple sclerosis is virus infection. At the same time, smoking without vitamin D will also increase the risk of the disease.

The latest research by scientists provides evidence for the relationship between the amount of salt in the diet and multiple sclerosis for the first time.
