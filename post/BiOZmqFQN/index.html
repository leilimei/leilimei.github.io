The origin of World Diabetes Day:

World Diabetes Day 'WDD: it was renamed as "United Nations Diabetes Day" in 2007. In 1991, in order to arouse the global awareness of diabetes and strengthen the publicity, education, prevention and monitoring of diabetes, it was jointly sponsored by the World Health Organization and the International Diabetes Federation. In order to commemorate the birth of Frederick banding, who discovered insulin (a therapy used to save the lives of diabetic patients) in 1922 together with Charles best, November 14 of each year was designated as World Diabetes Day.

Current situation of diabetic patients in China

According to the International Diabetes Federation, there are about 114 million people suffering from diabetes in China alone. It not only causes blindness, renal failure, amputation, heart disease and stroke, but also leads to the death of many patients, which is undoubtedly a medical problem.

Diabetes has been threatening our health and life for a long time, but most people's cognition of it is only "it's a disease, eat less sweet, and it's not easy to cure". In fact, the pathology of diabetes is more complex. To understand it, we must start from its characteristics.

Types of diabetes

Type 1 diabetes:

The onset age is young, mostly less than 30 years old. The onset of the disease is sudden. The symptoms of polydipsia, polyuria, polydipsia and emaciation are obvious. The blood glucose level is high. Many patients have ketoacidosis as the first symptom. The level of serum insulin and C-peptide is low, and ICA, IAA or GAD antibody can be positive. It is invalid to use oral medicine alone. Insulin is needed.

Type 2 diabetes:

It is common in middle-aged and elderly people, and the incidence rate of obesity is high, often accompanied by hypertension, dyslipidemia, arteriosclerosis and other diseases. In the early stage, there is no symptom, or only mild asthenia, thirst, and no obvious increase in blood glucose, which can only be diagnosed by glucose tolerance test. The serum insulin level was normal or increased in the early stage and low in the late stage.

The cause of the disease is complex and cannot be cured completely

The main causes of diabetes are:

\1. Too much pressure, mental tension and emotional disorder affect the hormone secretion in the body, resulting in the fluctuation of blood sugar.

\2. Familial heredity caused by the heredity of diabetes.

\3. Virus infection (such as enterovirus, mumps, myocarditis, etc.) damages islet cells and destroys human immune system.

\4. The long-term high-fat and high-sugar eating habits cause the body to be obese and the blood sugar to rise, so as to suffer from diabetes. (this is also one of the main causes of diabetes)

Diabetes will make the body worse, resulting in polyuria, polydipsia and other phenomena, and even if you eat more, you will still lose weight. Some will also have fatigue, itchy skin, vision loss, dizziness and other phenomena.

Generally speaking, diabetes is a chronic disease. The pathogenesis is caused by insulin resistance and insufficient insulin secretion. Some symptoms are irreversible. For example, after a cell is necrotic, it cannot regenerate and needs long-term treatment. On the basis of diet and exercise, if the patients are mainly resistant, the resistance will be improved; if the patients are mainly deficient, insulin will be supplemented. The disease is usually incurable, but it can improve symptoms.

However, with the development of medicine, oral antidiabetic drugs or insulin injection can also be used to treat severe diabetes. So people who are already sick don't have to be pessimistic.

How to prevent

Only by controlling the diet, reducing the intake of sugar, reasonably matching each meal, forming a good habit of regular exercise, and controlling the blood sugar in our body, can we effectively prevent the occurrence of diabetes.
