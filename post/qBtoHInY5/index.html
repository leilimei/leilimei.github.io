Protein supplement is an important part of healthy diet

2020-04-21

Summary:

There is no doubt that protein is an important part of a healthy diet. Research shows that protein can keep people full, help muscles recover after exercise, help lose weight, and even lower blood pressure. A recent study, published in the Journal of the American Heart Association, found that replacing calories from refined carbohydrates with proteins in 40 grams of milk or soybeans a day can moderately lower blood pressure.

Protein bar is a kind of nutritional supplement of protein, with various brands and flavors such as chocolate, dark chocolate, almond, vanilla, peanut butter and chocolate, oatmeal, etc. Many protein bars have a balanced carbohydrate, protein, and fat content, making them a good choice for snacks or after exercise. Some protein bars have a high sugar content, while others use glycols.

Jessica janc, a nutritionist at the National Association for sports nutrition, said not all protein bars are the same - many protein bar brands are rich in sugar, and they are more like candy bars than dietary supplements. The key, janc said, is that it's hard to choose a protein bar with the right structure. The protein should be at least half of the carbohydrate in the bar.

Likewise, ERICKA stachura of Boston suggests dieters look at sugar, protein and fiber levels when choosing protein bars. Dieters can choose a protein bar with 200 calories or less. Athletes who want to recover after exercise should choose a protein bar containing at least 20 grams of protein.

But will eating a protein bar every day lead to excessive protein intake? According to the Mayo Clinic, most Americans supplement twice as much protein as they need. Even without protein bars and other dietary supplements, athletes get more protein than they need. This is because athletes have higher calorie requirements. They burn a lot of calories and will eat more food and increase their protein intake. In addition, athletes can add more protein to their muscles.

For the average person, in order to keep healthy, the minimum protein required per day is 0.8g/kg. People who exercise regularly need a little more protein of about 1.1-1.5g/kg, while people who lift weights or take part in marathon or riding activities need 1.2-1.7g/kg, and more than 2.5g/kg is considered as excessive protein intake.

For women aged 40 to 50, protein needs to be increased to 1 to 1.2g/kg. This is because middle-aged women begin to lose muscle in the process of aging, and experts believe that increasing protein may help prevent this health condition. For those who already have muscular dystrophy, it may take between 1.2 and 1.5 g / kg per day.
