Summary:

Bio innovative medicine is a new industry in the pharmaceutical industry. The national "12th Five Year Plan" has determined the focus of the development of bio medicine. Polypeptide drugs have been widely used in the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of cancer, hepatitis, diabetes, AIDS and other diseases due to their wide indications, high safety and significant efficacy. The targeting of antibody drug coupling (ADC) drugs combined with monoclonal antibodies and the high cytotoxicity of small molecule drugs can effectively kill tumor cells. In preclinical research and even human clinical research, it has achieved good anti-tumor effect, and become one of the hot areas of biological drug research.

1、 Polypeptide drugs

Polypeptide is a kind of compound formed by the connection of multiple amino acids through peptide bond, which is usually composed of 10-100 amino acid molecules. Its connection mode is the same as that of protein, and its relative molecular weight is less than 10000. Polypeptides are ubiquitous in organisms. Up to now, tens of thousands of polypeptides have been found in organisms. They are widely involved in and regulate the functional activities of various systems, organs, tissues and cells in the body, and play an important role in life activities. At present, there are three main methods for the preparation of polypeptide drugs: chemical synthesis, gene recombination and extraction from driven plants. The solid-phase synthesis technology in chemical synthesis has greatly promoted the development of peptide drugs. Gene recombination refers to the process of exchanging and recombining DNA fragments due to the breaking and connecting of different DNA chains, so as to form new DNA molecules. Gene recombination is mainly used in the preparation of long peptides. According to statistics, in 2012, there were 128 peptide drugs in clinical research, including 40 in phase I, 74 in phase II and 14 in phase III.

In the world, peptide drugs are mainly used in the fields of chronic diseases, tumors and rare diseases, among which there are many kinds of heavyweight. With the expiration of patents for multiple peptide varieties, peptide generic drugs will usher in development opportunities, while upstream peptide API manufacturers will also benefit from the growth of the generic drug market. At present, the global peptide drug market has exceeded 20 billion US dollars, accounting for about 2% of the total market share of medical drugs, maintaining a growth rate of about 10%. In the world, heavyweight peptide drugs are mainly concentrated in the fields of tumor, chronic disease and rare disease. In China, peptide drugs are mainly used for immune assistance and gastrointestinal hemostasis, such as thymopentin and somatostatin. With the expiration of patents for multiple peptide heavyweight products, generic drugs will have an opportunity.

1.1 global market scale

With the development of biotechnology and peptide synthesis technology, more and more peptide drugs have been approved for clinical treatment. Polypeptide drugs have been widely used in the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of cancer, hepatitis, diabetes, AIDS and other diseases due to their wide indications, high safety and significant efficacy. At present, the average research and development cycle of peptide drugs is 0.7 years less than that of chemical drugs, and the proportion of drug-forming is also higher than that of chemical drugs. The number of peptide drugs on the market in the world is increasing year by year. At the same time, a large number of peptide drugs have entered clinical research. In recent years, the compound growth rate of the global peptide drug market is more than 12%, higher than the overall drug market. The market size is nearly 20 billion US dollars (as shown in the figure below), and it is expected to reach 25.4 billion US dollars by 2018. The overall scale of peptide drugs is still small, but with the maturity of synthesis technology and the development of preparation technology, peptide drugs have a larger development space.

1.2 current situation of domestic market

Compared with the developed countries, there is still a big gap in peptide drug industry in China, and the product structure is also different. At present, the proportion of immune enhancement products in peptide drug market in China is large, such as thymopentin, thymus method, etc., while the share of anti-tumor, diabetes, rare diseases and other varieties is small, the market is not mature, and there is a great development space. Compared with the international market, China's peptide drug market is still in a rapid growth period. On the one hand, because the peptide drug market in China is not mature, and some large varieties such as cancer and diabetes have less market share, there is a huge growth space; on the other hand, the increasing degree of aging, changes in living habits and environmental deterioration and other factors make the prevalence of cancer, chronic diseases and other factors increase the demand for drugs.

1.3 analysis on the gap and problems of domestic peptide drug industry

At present, the structural problems affecting the development of the pharmaceutical industry are increasingly prominent, mainly reflected in:

(1) China's pharmaceutical enterprises have serious homogeneous competition, weak innovation ability, lack of independent innovation and high value-added products.

(2) The structural problems of long-term accumulation, such as insufficient sustainable development, weak international competitiveness, serious environmental pollution and large energy consumption, are increasingly prominent.

(3) The industrial concentration is low, and the profitability is not strong; the situation of many, small, scattered and low has not been completely changed, and the degree of scale, industrialization and intensification is not high. Large enterprises only account for 1.83% of the total number of enterprises.

(4) Domestic enterprises account for 80% of the total number of enterprises, and the total profit only accounts for 61.86% of the whole industry. The profitability of domestic enterprises is significantly lower than that of Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan and foreign-funded enterprises.

1.4 opportunities and challenges in peptide field

The incidence rate of diabetes, cancer and other diseases is increasing with the aging of population. The multidrug resistance of bacteria also needs the application of new anti infective drugs. Polypeptide drugs have a wide range of indications, which are suitable for endocrine and metabolic regulation system, anti-tumor, anti infection and other treatment fields, with high specificity and high safety.

Parody has been the hottest word in recent years, but most of them are small molecule drugs, but the big molecule drugs are ignored, the main reason is that it is difficult to break through the technical barriers. At present, except for glutathione, thymopentin, leuprorelin and octreotide, all polypeptides sold in China are imported, and the market share of imported products is nearly 80%. At present, peptide synthesis in China is still in its infancy, and the products that can be produced independently in China are all short peptides with no more than 10 amino acids.

The research and development of peptide products is different from that of generic drugs, which requires a little accumulation and long-term challenges. If we still do the three kinds of drugs as before, it is difficult to get results. At present, most of the peptides on the market are short peptides with less than 40 amino acids. The difficulty of solid-phase synthesis is impurity control and purification. According to statistics, the probability of approval of peptide drugs entering clinical research is 23% - 26%, significantly higher than that of small molecule drugs. Therefore, the risk of developing peptide drugs is relatively small. Due to the limitations of technical conditions and equipment, peptide drugs in China are still in the initial stage, and there are not many peptide drugs in clinical application, so there is a huge development space in the future.

2、 Antibody coupled drugs (ADC)

Antibody drug conjugates (ADC) is a kind of drug which can couple compound drugs with antibodies through linker, and then reduce the non-specific systemic toxicity of common drugs in chemotherapy. It has become a research and development hotspot of monoclonal antibody for tumor treatment. ADCs are transported by blood to recognize cancer cells with surface antigens. The antibody part of ADCs is engulfed into cancer cells after binding with antigens and further sheared in lysosomes to release compounds with killing effect (acting on DNA or microtubules). After cell death and lysis, the toxin molecules will be released to other nearby tumor cells to further kill. The research and development of ADCs drugs include: appropriate drug target, highly specific antibody, ideal coupons, and efficient drugs.

From the perspective of antibody technology development cycle: ADC antibody drugs will be concentrated in 2016-2020. First of all, the approval of kadcyla and adcetis has proved the maturity of ADC technology. Secondly, according to the historical rule that 15 years' incubation period is needed from the birth of antibody preparation technology to the marketing of corresponding drugs, ADC technology has basically completed 15 years' incubation period since its birth in 2000. Therefore, we judge that the ADC antibody drug field will focus on the outbreak in 2016-2020. At present, there are about 30 kinds of ADC drugs entering the clinical development stage. But there are only four drugs for solid tumors. The main reason is that it is difficult for the antibody to penetrate the capillary endothelial layer and the extracellular space of the tumor to reach the deep part of the solid tumor.

From the perspective of the varieties under development by FDA, most of the ADC antibody drugs are in clinical stage II / III, which is expected to be approved in a centralized way. At present, most of the ADC antibody drugs under research are in the late clinical stage. We expect that the ADC drugs will be approved in the next 2-3 years due to the significant efficacy of the ADC drugs and the high probability of prior approval. Antibody coupled drugs are a well deserved new growth point of tumor targeting drugs. Relevant reports show that ADC market will develop rapidly in the next 10 years. It is estimated that there will be 7-10 ADC drugs on the market by 2024, and the ADC market size will reach 10 billion US dollars in 2024. However, the two ADC drugs on the market have shown a rapid growth since they have been on the market, and become a new growth point of targeted drugs. Take Roche's kadcyla for example. It went public in 2013, and its sales reached 20 million dollars in the first quarter of 2013.

From the perspective of ADC efficacy: as an antibody small molecule drug coupling agent, ADC drugs can significantly improve the survival period of tumor patients compared with the combination therapy of antibody and chemical drugs alone. ADC medicine itself is a good concept, but its technology transformation is still looking forward to a greater breakthrough. As the target of missile and small molecule as the warhead of toxic molecule, two key points have been prepared, but the antibody needs to be internalized effectively, linker needs to be released efficiently and maintain stability outside the cell, which still has many difficulties, resulting in the small molecule entering the target cell and having little effect, which brings great potential problems of effectiveness and safety.

At present, most of the ADC drugs developed in the forefront use traditional coupling technology, the biggest disadvantage is that the product is a mixture of different drug molecules per antibody; it can not achieve specific location coupling drugs, and the clinical evaluation data is not uniform. In order to solve this problem, the technology of directional coupling has become a hot topic for pharmaceutical companies. Using directed coupling technology can make each antibody carry the same number of drug molecules, and get a uniform ADC drug, which makes the clinical effect more stable and effective. Another trend is that the development of antibody drugs is from monovalent drugs to multivalent drugs. It is expected to couple several small molecules on one antibody to improve the efficacy of drugs. This requires more perfect antibody coupling technology, at least two or more technologies need to be adjusted, and a variety of different coupling groups need to be designed. Finally, through linker diversification to modify the drug link, the multivalent coupling of ADC drugs can be realized.
